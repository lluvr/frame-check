Operator-actionable kit for recruiting one or two domain-distant practitioners to stress-test the v2 architecture against their lived practice. Closes the cross-curator validation gap named in `FRAME_DIVERGENCE_v2.md` §3.5 as "the next test" for the L0 doctrine and the broader DOF taxonomy.

**Author:** Lovro Lucic.
**Date:** 2026-04-25.
**Status:** v1 draft, operator-pending recruitment activation.
**Companion:** `FRAME_DIVERGENCE_v2.md` §3.4-§3.5 (cross-cutting receiver-state principles + honest scope; cross-curator validation named).

---

## 1. Why cross-curator validation matters

The v2 architecture is single-author. Layer 0 receiver-state dimensions, the L1 axis taxonomy, the L2 composite catalog, the L2.5 chain library, and the lived-experience anchor criteria across 19 retrofitted FVS entries all reflect one curator's cognitive habits and lived practice.

Single-author bias is the load-bearing risk for v2's structural-validity claim. A different curator with different lived practice may:
- Produce different L1 axes (or call my axes wrong)
- Surface L2 composites I did not name
- Use chains I did not catalog
- Find lived-experience anchor patterns inconsistent with my framing

If cross-curator data confirms the architecture roughly fits a domain-distant practitioner's lived practice (50 percent or more of their lived examples tag cleanly against existing axes; fewer than three categorical missing axes), v2 is robust. If less than 30 percent tag cleanly or 3+ missing axes surface, v2 needs major revision.

Either outcome is a strong empire signal. Pass strengthens the architecture's claim to generality. Fail produces a concrete revision target rather than a moving-target uncertainty. Both outcomes ship.

## 2. Recruitment target

**Goal:** 1 to 2 domain-distant practitioners willing to spend 90 to 120 minutes on the engagement.

**Domain-distant** means: not in AI/ML, not in cognitive science academia, ideally working in a frame-rich field where perspective-shift is operative in their daily practice.

**Practitioner profile:** working professional with 5+ years of domain experience; comfortable articulating their reasoning process; interested in providing structured critique; not seeking compensation as primary motivation (small honorarium offered, not the main attraction).

## 3. Candidate practitioner domains

Six domains where frame work is genuinely operative and the practitioner's lived data is most likely to stress-test v2:

### 3.1 Strategy consultant (Big-3 or boutique)

**Why:** consulting work is frame-shifting under client constraints. Strategy consultants explicitly reframe client problems as part of their methodology. They are likely to recognize chain operations (premortem, stakeholder analysis, scenario planning) and have lived examples of frame-divergence shifts that produced different outcomes.

**What to surface:** their experience with explicit frame-shift methodology (six hats, scenario planning, premortem); whether the L2.5 chain library captures or misses their toolkit; whether the L0 receiver-state doctrine matches their experience of client-readiness for difficult frames.

### 3.2 Clinical or counseling psychologist

**Why:** therapy is frame work at the receiver-state level. Therapists work explicitly with how clients receive frames (resistance, defenses, openness). Their lived data is dense in L0 receiver-state observations.

**What to surface:** whether the L0 dimensions (engagement state, conditioning, identity coupling) match their clinical observation; whether reframing techniques in cognitive-behavioral therapy or related modalities map to L1 axes or L2 composites; whether the lived-experience anchor criteria for FVS-013 Oracle Frame, FVS-015 Efficiency Frame, or FVS-018 Scope Narrowing produce recall.

### 3.3 Senior investment analyst (PM, hedge fund analyst, or equivalent)

**Why:** investment work requires frame-divergence under decision constraint. Analysts run explicit counter-frame analyses (bear case, base case, bull case) and have lived data on which frame shifts produced different positions.

**What to surface:** whether the L1 VALUE-WHY axis matches their experience of value-criterion shifts; whether the adversarial chain (optimist > realist > adversary > premortem > mitigation > commitment) captures or misses their decision-framework toolkit; whether FVS-009 Risk Frame, FVS-010 Completeness Illusion, FVS-016 Authority by Citation produce concrete lived recall.

### 3.4 Senior product designer or design researcher

**Why:** design research is reframing user problems under design constraint. Designers work with multiple stakeholder perspectives, persona-shifts, and constraint variation as core methodology.

**What to surface:** whether the L2 composite catalog (multi-stakeholder dialogue, persona-in-history, edge-case generator) matches their methodology; whether FVS-011 Stakeholder Frame, FVS-014 Temporal Anchoring, FVS-019 Narrative Coherence map to their work.

### 3.5 Sport coach (high-performance, college, or pro level)

**Why:** coaching is receiver-state work plus frame-shift work under high stakes. Coaches manage athlete L0 (mental state, identity, readiness) and apply explicit reframes (process vs outcome, internal vs external locus, growth vs fixed mindset).

**What to surface:** whether the L0 doctrine matches their athlete-state work; whether identity coupling and frictionless-vs-effortful conditioning produce recall; whether the human-AI asymmetry on identity-detachment lands as a recognizable structural claim.

### 3.6 Working artist (visual, sound, film, or writing)

**Why:** art is frame work as the medium. Artists work with literal perspective (visual), constraint shifts (composition), persona-and-stakeholder work (writing), narrative coherence (film). Their lived practice is structurally-different from analytical-pragmatic work.

**What to surface:** whether the architecture's honest scope ("perspective-shift in pragmatic problem-solving") holds when stress-tested against creative practice; whether L1 CONSTRAINT and SALIENCE-WHAT axes match their work; whether the architecture survives a domain that intentionally values constraint over efficiency.

## 5. Engagement protocol

### 5.1 Pre-engagement (maintainer-side, 30 minutes)

1. Send prep brief to recruited practitioner. Brief is `FRAME_DIVERGENCE_v2_SUMMARY.md` (5-7 pages). `ANCHOR_AUTHORSHIP_METHODOLOGY_v1.md` is offered as optional companion reading for practitioners who want to apply the introspective protocol when surfacing lived examples; Phase 2 of the engagement (§5.2) is consistent with the methodology's surface-deepen-corroborate phases applied to multiple candidates rather than one.
2. Practitioner reads at their own pace (estimated 15-20 minutes for the summary; an additional 30-40 minutes if engaging the anchor methodology).
3. Practitioner is asked to come prepared with 8-12 lived examples from their practice where frame-shift produced different outcomes. Format per example: 1-2 sentences each, brief.

### 5.2 Live engagement (90 minutes; can be split into 2 sessions)

**Phase 1 (10 min):** Practitioner-led intro. Practitioner names their domain, their typical framing work, what frames mean in their practice. Operator listens.

**Phase 2 (40 min):** Practitioner walks through their 8-12 lived examples. Operator captures each in protocol §3.2 format (Decision; Context; Stakes; Current framing). Operator does NOT tag during this phase; just captures.

**Phase 3 (30 min):** Operator tags each example against v2 architecture (which L1 axis was operative; L2 composite or L2.5 chain involved; L0 receiver-state observations). Operator surfaces tagging to practitioner; practitioner critiques each tag.

**Phase 4 (10 min):** Aggregate critique. Practitioner names: (a) tags they agreed with; (b) tags they disagreed with; (c) frames in their lived data that did not fit any axis (missing-axis candidates); (d) vocabulary they use that v2 does not name.

### 5.3 Post-engagement (maintainer-side, 1-2 hours)

1. Operator writes structured findings document. Format:
   - Practitioner profile (anonymized if requested)
   - Lived examples (anonymized as needed)
   - Tag agreement matrix (which v2 elements survived; which did not)
   - Missing-axis candidates with practitioner attribution
   - Vocabulary-gap inventory
   - Operator's revision recommendations for v2

2. Findings document committed as `data/cross_curator/practitioner_<N>_findings_v1.md`.
3. Findings shared with practitioner for review and approval before public attribution.
4. Honorarium paid within 7 days.

## 7. Recruitment timeline

- Week 1-2: operator drafts list of 20-30 candidate practitioners across the 6 domains. Aims for 5+ per domain to allow for response-rate attrition. Lists local/national/international as appropriate to operator's network.
- Week 1-2: operator sends Template A or B per candidate (Template B preferred when mutual contact exists).
- Week 3-4: response collection. Expected response rate is 5-15 percent for cold contact (Template A); 25-40 percent for warm intro (Template B). Aim for 1-2 commitments.
- Week 4-5: pre-engagement (prep brief sent, scheduling).
- Week 5-8: live engagements (1-2 sessions per practitioner).
- Week 9-10: post-engagement findings documents written and reviewed.
- Week 10-12: findings integrated into v2.x increments where supported.

Total elapsed: 10-12 weeks for first 1-2 practitioners. Subsequent rounds at faster cadence once recruitment template proven.

## 8. Compensation model

**Honorarium:** $300 USD per practitioner per engagement.

**Why $300:** signals serious request without crossing into "this is paid consulting work" territory. Most practitioners in target domains earn $300+ per hour as consulting rate; the honorarium is symbolic of seriousness and respect for time, not market-rate compensation.

**Alternative:** equivalent charitable donation to recipient's choice of registered charity. Some practitioners prefer this for tax or symbolic reasons.

**Budget impact:** 2 practitioners x $300 = $600. Within `STRATEGY.md` §12 budget envelope ($1K/year normal); if more practitioners engaged, $5K exception trigger applies and operator BDFL override with recorded reasoning is required.

---

## 9. Attribution + IP

**Practitioner attribution:** practitioner's choice. Three options:
- Public attribution (named in findings document and any subsequent v2 increments that cite their data)
- Pseudonymous attribution (consistent pseudonym across documents)
- Anonymous (described only by domain category)

**Findings IP:** findings document is co-owned. Practitioner has veto over what is published. Operator commits to no embargo beyond practitioner-requested timeframe.

**v2 spec attribution:** if cross-curator findings produce v2.x revisions, the practitioners who contributed are named in the v2 spec changelog (per their attribution preference) at the next spec increment.

---

## 10. Honest scope

This kit is operator-actionable but operator-execution-pending. The kit specifies recruitment targets, outreach templates, engagement protocol, findings format, timeline, compensation, and attribution. What it does not do: recruit the practitioners (maintainer-side), conduct the engagements (maintainer-side), write the findings (maintainer-side, post-engagement), or integrate the findings into v2.x revisions (maintainer-side after findings).

The cross-curator gap is the named next test in `FRAME_DIVERGENCE_v2.md` §3.5. Without cross-curator data, v2's structural validity claim rests on single-author construction. With one or two practitioners' findings integrated, v2 transitions from single-author proposal to cross-validated initial proposal. Empire claim strengthens accordingly.
