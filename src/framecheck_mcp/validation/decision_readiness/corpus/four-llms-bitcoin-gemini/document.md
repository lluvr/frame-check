For a 35-year-old saving for retirement, my direct recommendation is:

**No, Bitcoin is generally not a good primary investment for a significant portion of your retirement savings.** However, a **very small, speculative allocation (e.g., 1-5%)** could be considered *if* you fully understand and are comfortable with the extreme risks involved.

Here's the reasoning:

**Why it's generally NOT a good primary retirement investment:**

1.  **Extreme Volatility:** Bitcoin is notoriously volatile. Its price can swing wildly (up or down 20-50% or more) in short periods. While a 35-year-old has a long time horizon, this level of volatility makes it incredibly difficult to rely on for consistent, long-term wealth accumulation needed for retirement. A major downturn close to retirement age could devastate your savings.
2.  **Lack of Intrinsic Value & Speculative Nature:** Unlike a company stock (which has earnings, assets, and cash flow) or a bond (which pays interest), Bitcoin's value is largely speculative, driven by supply/demand, adoption, sentiment, and perceived future utility as "digital gold" or a payment network. There's no underlying business or asset to anchor its price.
3.  **Regulatory Uncertainty:** Governments worldwide are still grappling with how to regulate cryptocurrencies. Potential bans, severe restrictions, or unfavorable tax policies could significantly impact Bitcoin's value and accessibility.
4.  **Security Risks:** While the Bitcoin network itself is robust, individual holdings are vulnerable to hacking, scams, or loss if private keys are not managed perfectly. Exchange failures are also a risk.
5.  **Unproven Long-Term Store of Value:** While proponents argue it's a hedge against inflation and a store of value, Bitcoin has only existed for a little over a decade. It hasn't weathered multiple economic cycles or geopolitical crises in the same way traditional assets like gold or real estate have.

**Why a small, speculative allocation MIGHT be considered (with extreme caution):**

1.  **Potential for High Returns:** Bitcoin has demonstrated incredible growth over its lifetime. If it continues to gain wider adoption and acceptance, a small investment could theoretically see significant appreciation.
2.  **Long Time Horizon:** As a 35-year-old, you have 25-30+ years until retirement. This allows more time to recover from potential downturns, *if* Bitcoin ultimately proves to be a long-term viable asset.
3.  **Diversification (Limited):** Some argue Bitcoin can act as an uncorrelated asset to traditional markets, potentially offering a small diversification benefit. However, its correlation can shift.
4.  **Inflation Hedge (Potential):** Its fixed supply (21 million coins) leads some to believe it's a strong hedge against inflation and currency debasement, though this is still debated and not fully proven over extended periods.

**Key Takeaway for a 35-year-old:**

Your primary retirement strategy should focus on a diversified portfolio of traditional assets like low-cost index funds or ETFs covering global stocks and bonds. These assets, while having their own risks, have a proven track record of long-term growth and are generally less volatile than Bitcoin.
