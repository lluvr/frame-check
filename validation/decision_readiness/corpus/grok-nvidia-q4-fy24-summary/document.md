### NVIDIA Reports Record Q4 Revenue of $22.1 Billion, Up 265% Year-Over-Year

NVIDIA (NASDAQ: NVDA) announced financial results for its fourth quarter and fiscal year 2024, ended January 28, 2024.

Q4 revenue reached a record $22.1 billion, up 22% from the prior quarter and 265% from the year-ago period. GAAP earnings per diluted share were $4.93, up 33% quarter-over-quarter and 765% year-over-year. Non-GAAP earnings per diluted share stood at $5.16, up 28% from Q3 and 486% from Q4 of fiscal 2023.

For fiscal 2024, revenue totaled $60.9 billion, a 126% increase year-over-year. GAAP EPS was $11.93, up 586%, while non-GAAP EPS was $12.96, up 288%.

Data Center revenue hit a record $18.4 billion in Q4, up 27% from Q3 and 409% year-over-year.

CEO Jensen Huang stated, "Accelerated computing and generative AI have hit the tipping point," citing surging demand from cloud providers, enterprises, and verticals like auto, financial services, and healthcare. He noted NVIDIA RTX now serves 100 million gamers and creators.

NVIDIA declared a quarterly dividend of $0.04 per share, payable March 27, 2024, to shareholders of record on March 6.
