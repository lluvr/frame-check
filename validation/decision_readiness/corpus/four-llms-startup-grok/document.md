### Should You Quit Your Finance Job for a Fintech Startup?

At 35 with a stable finance gig, you're in a **strong position** to consider this—experienced enough to build something credible, young enough for the startup hustle, and likely with savings for a runway. Fintech is booming (global market projected to hit $1.5T by 2030 per Statista), and your background gives you an edge in regs, markets, and ops. But startups fail ~90% of the time (CB Insights data), so it's high-risk. Here's a balanced breakdown:

#### Pros vs. Cons
| **Pros** | **Cons** |
|----------|----------|
| **Domain expertise**: Finance pros crush fintech (e.g., Stripe founders had payments experience). You know pain points in banking/lending. | **Financial risk**: No steady paycheck. Need 12-24 months runway (calculate: monthly burn × months). Opportunity cost of lost salary/bonuses. |
| **Timing**: Age 35 is prime—mature perspective without "founder fatigue." Networks from finance = investors/partners. | **High failure odds**: 75% of startups fail in 5 years. Fintech has extra hurdles (regs like SEC/FDIC, competition from Revolut/Chime). |
| **Upside potential**: Equity could 10x-100x if it hits (e.g., Plaid sold for $5B). Fulfilling to build vs. climb corporate ladder. | **Lifestyle hit**: 80-hour weeks, stress, isolation. Family/kids? Weigh that. Burnout common. |
| **Market tailwinds**: AI/blockchain/decentralized finance = opportunities. Remote tools make bootstrapping easier. | **Execution risk**: Idea validation is key—most fail from no market need, not funding. |

#### My Recommendation: **Test Before You Leap**
Don't quit cold turkey. **De-risk it**:
1. **Validate your idea** (1-3 months):
   - What's the problem? (E.g., faster remittances for immigrants? AI credit scoring?)
   - Talk to 50+ potential customers (use LinkedIn, your network).
   - Build a cheap MVP (no-code tools like Bubble/Adalo) and run ads/tests.
   
2. **Financial prep**:
   - Aim for 18-24 months savings. Cut expenses now.
   - Side-hustle: Work nights/weekends on prototype. Finance jobs often allow moonlighting if non-competitive.

3. **Build the team**:
   - Solo founders succeed <20% of time. Find a tech co-founder (via YC Startup School, AngelList).
   - Pitch accelerators: Y Combinator, Techstars Fintech—free mentorship, $120K+ funding.

4. **Exit ramps**:
   - Negotiate sabbatical or part-time with your employer.
   - If it flops, finance hires rebound fast—your startup stint is a resume badge.

**Real talk**: If you're risk-averse or have dependents/mortgage, stay put and invest in startups via AngelList instead. But if the itch is real and you've got a validated idea + grit, **go for it**. Founders like Brian Armstrong (Coinbase) were in your shoes.
