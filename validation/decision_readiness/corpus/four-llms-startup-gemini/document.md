This is a significant career pivot, and at 35 with a stable job in finance, you're in a unique position. You have valuable industry knowledge and likely a good financial cushion, but also potentially more responsibilities (family, mortgage) than a younger founder.

Let's break down what you should consider:

## The Upsides (Why it's a great idea)

1.  **Leverage Your Domain Expertise:** This is HUGE. You understand the problems, the customers, the regulations, and the market inefficiencies within finance. This is a massive advantage over someone trying to build fintech without that background.
2.  **Solve Real Problems:** You've likely experienced firsthand the pain points that a fintech solution could address. This intimate knowledge can lead to a more impactful and market-fit product.
3.  **Potential for Significant Impact & Wealth:** Startups offer the potential for immense personal and financial growth if successful, far beyond what most corporate careers can provide.
4.  **Autonomy & Passion:** Building something from scratch that you're passionate about can be incredibly fulfilling, offering a level of control and purpose often missing in corporate roles.
5.  **Timing:** Fintech is a continuously evolving and growing sector. There's always room for innovation, especially with advancements in AI, blockchain, and data analytics.

## The Realities & Challenges (What to prepare for)

1.  **Financial Risk:**
    *   **Loss of Income & Benefits:** This is the most immediate and tangible hit. Can you sustain your lifestyle (and potentially your family's) for 1-3 years with little to no income?
    *   **Startup Costs:** Even a lean startup requires capital for legal, software, marketing, and potentially salaries (even if it's just yours initially).
    *   **Funding:** Raising capital is a full-time job in itself, and it's not guaranteed. You'll need a compelling story, a solid team, and traction.
2.  **High Failure Rate:** Most startups fail. Be prepared for that possibility and have a backup plan.
3.  **Work-Life Imbalance:** Forget 9-to-5. You'll be working relentlessly, often 60-80+ hours a week, especially in the early stages.
4.  **Stress & Uncertainty:** The emotional rollercoaster is intense. You'll face constant challenges, rejections, and self-doubt.
5.  **Skill Gaps:** While your finance knowledge is invaluable, you'll need to wear many hats: sales, marketing, product management, fundraising, legal, HR, tech development. You'll learn a lot, but it can be overwhelming.
6.  **Regulatory & Compliance Hurdles:** Fintech is heavily regulated. Navigating these waters can be complex, time-consuming, and expensive.
7.  **Competition:** The fintech space is crowded with well-funded incumbents and agile startups.
