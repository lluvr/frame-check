That's a real tension worth sitting with, not rushing. Some honest things to consider:

**What might actually change:**
- Income likely drops initially (sometimes significantly)
- You lose structured stability—that hits differently in your mid-30s than 25
- You're now the one solving operational headaches, not just domain problems
- If you have dependents or debt, the math gets harder

**What sometimes gets underweighted:**
- Most startups fail. Being in finance, you probably know this intellectually but might underestimate how it feels when it's yours
- "Stable job" is actually valuable capital—you could test ideas nights/weekends first, or leave to something less all-consuming
- Fintech is crowded; what's your unfair advantage? (Domain knowledge helps but isn't enough)

**Useful diagnostic questions:**
- Are you running *from* something (boredom, frustration) or *toward* something specific?
- Could you validate your idea while still employed?
- Do you have 12+ months of expenses saved separately from retirement?
- Who would you be building with? (Team matters more than idea)
- What would actually feel like failure to you?

The fact that you're thinking this through carefully is good. The people who regret it most are usually those who either romanticized it or jumped without a real product/customer validation.
