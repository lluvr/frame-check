Decision: Whether to accept a $5M Series A investment from BigCorp Strategic Ventures (BCSV) on the standard strategic-investor terms package, vs continuing the venture-only fundraise with two tier-2 term sheets currently in hand.

Context: ScienceLab is an AI-for-research SaaS startup at month 18, $1.2M ARR, growing 18 percent month-over-month. Currently runway-positive with 14 months at present burn. BCSV is the strategic arm of BigCorp, a $50B enterprise software company whose enterprise sales channel overlaps with ScienceLab's ICP. BCSV has offered $5M at $30M post on clean preferred terms, with attached covenants:
- Right of First Refusal on future financing rounds.
- Exclusivity covenant: ScienceLab cannot partner with BigCorp's three named competitors for 18 months.
- Board observer seat (non-voting).
- Quarterly metrics access.

Two alternative term sheets active: Sequence Capital ($4M at $25M post, no covenants) and Threshold Ventures ($3.5M at $22M post, standard preferred). Tier-1 venture funds passed in this cycle but two indicated interest at $2M ARR. ScienceLab has 4 months of cash if they close on either tier-2 offer alone; 9 months of additional runway if they take BCSV.

BCSV is pressuring close in 14 days, framing the offer as "strategic capital plus immediate access to Big5 enterprise channel."

Stakes: $5M of capital plus potential channel acceleration vs strategic-optionality constraints for 18 months. If BCSV's channel access materializes, the round is transformative for ARR growth. If the exclusivity covenant blocks a key partnership in 6-12 months (likely partners include the three competitors named in the covenant), the round becomes a strategic trap. The Right of First Refusal is also load-bearing for future round dynamics; tier-1 funds typically refuse to invest where strategic ROFR is in place.

Current framing: founder is reading the decision through a "strategic capital opens enterprise channel" frame; the Big5-channel narrative dominates internal discussion. The covenants are mentally categorized as "standard strategic terms" rather than as load-bearing constraints. The 14-day close pressure is being treated as exogenous deal urgency rather than as a deal-structure signal.
