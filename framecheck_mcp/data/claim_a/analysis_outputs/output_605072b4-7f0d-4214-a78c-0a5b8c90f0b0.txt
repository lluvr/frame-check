### Structured Commitment Statement

#### 1. Recommended Commitment
Proceed with conditions. The deep integration with DominantPlatform offers a high-upside path to accelerate growth in a dominant channel (leveraging the existing 45% customer sourcing to potentially reach 70% while accessing the data API for retention gains), but only if key safeguards are secured to address the power imbalance and failure modes identified in the premortem. This reframes the COO's "necessity" lens by treating the 60% dominance as capturable without full surrender, prioritizing mitigations over shallow integration (which caps upsides) or refusal (which risks a 50% sourced-customer drop without buffers). Proceed only after negotiating and verifying the conditions below; if they cannot be met within 4 weeks, default to shallow integration to preserve optionality.

#### 2. Specific Conditions to Proceed
These conditions are directly anchored to the premortem failure modes (a)-(d) and the corresponding mitigations from Step 5. They must be contractually locked in via negotiation, with COO-level escalation if needed, and verified through a pre-commitment audit (e.g., term sheet review by legal/finance within 2 weeks). Failure to secure any one halts progression.

- **Condition for (a) Dependency Lock-In and Customer Exodus**: Negotiate a phased exclusivity covenant limited to 12 months (instead of 24) or tied to performance milestones (e.g., enforceable only if DominantPlatform-sourced customers exceed 60% of total ARR). Pre-emptively, allocate 10% of engineering resources to a 1-month pilot enhancing integrations with the two top alternative platforms, quantifying the 55% direct/small-platform base's resilience (target: 20% YoY growth in direct acquisitions via A/B testing). This builds a diversification buffer, reducing churn risk from 50-60% to under 30% and preserving ARR above $22M.

- **Condition for (b) UI Approval Gridlock Stifles Product Evolution**: Secure a fast-track approval protocol capping UI reviews at 30 days, with self-certification allowed for non-core changes (e.g., scheduling tool updates not impacting data flows). Form a dedicated "UI autonomy squad" (2-3 engineers, 20% of product budget) to develop and beta-test modular features with 100 existing creators over 1 month, ensuring 80% of planned 18-month iterations (5-7 updates) can deploy independently. This maintains retention above 85% and avoids the $60M valuation drop by retaining agility in content-management tools for 10K-1M followers.

- **Condition for (c) Revenue Share Escalation and Cash Flow Collapse**: Implement a tiered revenue share structure: 18% up to $2M annually (on new LTV only), dropping to 12% thereafter, or indexed to verified acquisition uplift from "Featured" placement. Build and share a 24-month financial model (projecting 15% market dips on $25M ARR) in negotiations within 10 days, backed by benchmarks from the 70% dependency analysis. Simultaneously, reallocate 5% of quarterly marketing spend ($500K) to a 3-month direct acquisition pilot (e.g., LinkedIn campaigns targeting 10K-1M creators), targeting ROI to offset the $4M baseline cost and ensure cash flow positivity, preventing stagnation at $22M ARR.

- **Condition for (d) Data API Exploitation and Competitive Undermining**: Embed data access guarantees, including minimum query limits (1M/month on creator behavior analytics), audit rights for throttling, and a non-compete clause barring DominantPlatform from using shared LTV metrics for in-house tools. Over 2 months pre-commitment, launch a proprietary data initiative via internal surveys and analytics on 20% of the 55% independent base (target: 50K data points on engagement patterns), reducing API reliance by 60%. This sustains ARR above $23M by ensuring reliable insights without 30% user diversion.

Upon meeting all conditions, execute the 24-month commitment, monitoring quarterly via a cross-functional dashboard (tracking metrics like sourced-customer %, retention, and share payouts) with a 6-month review clause to exit if thresholds slip (e.g., dependency >65%).

#### 3. Deferral Triggers (If Conditions Unmet)
If negotiations fail to secure the above conditions within 4 weeks (e.g., DominantPlatform rejects phased exclusivity or data guarantees outright), defer the decision and pursue shallow integration as an interim step. This would change the decision toward proceeding only if: (i) DominantPlatform provides concessions based on new competitive intelligence (e.g., evidence of alternative platforms gaining >10% channel share, unlocking better bargaining); or (ii) ContentCo's internal pilots demonstrate outsized resilience in the 55% base (e.g., direct acquisition yielding >25% YoY growth, reducing the 50% drop risk from refusal). Reassess in 6 months with updated industry data on dependency trends.

#### 4. Acknowledgment of Unmitigated Concerns
Not all adversary and premortem risks are fully addressed, even with conditions. Specifically: (i) The inherent market power imbalance (60% dominance) leaves residual vulnerability to adversarial policy shifts, such as mid-term share hikes or "Featured" revocation, which could still evaporate 40-50% of sourced revenue despite caps—historical consolidations suggest a 20-30% chance of this materializing unmitigated. (ii) Second-order effects like talent flight from integration frustrations or market perception as a "DominantPlatform satellite" remain partially unaddressed, potentially eroding the 55% independent base by 15-20% through morale hits, as no condition fully insulates cultural/branding autonomy. These gaps underscore that while upsides are unlocked, the $4M cost and 70% dependency risk retain a tail-end exposure to catastrophic lock-in, favoring vigilant monitoring over complacency.
