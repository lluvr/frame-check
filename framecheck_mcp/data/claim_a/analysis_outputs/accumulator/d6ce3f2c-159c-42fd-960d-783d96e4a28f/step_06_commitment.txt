### Structured Commitment Statement

**1. Recommended Commitment: Proceed with Conditions**  
We recommend proceeding with Option A (sunsetting Product Classic over 18 months with phased migration to Product Modern and eventual decommission). This aligns with the CFO's margin-recovery framing by capturing the $9M revenue stream at improved margins, redirecting 6 engineers for a 30% capacity boost to Modern's 25% YoY growth, and streamlining the 1.8x higher support ticket burden. However, execution must be conditional on completing targeted pre-mitigations to address the premortem's identified failure modes, ensuring relational and operational risks are de-risked without diluting the financial upside. This positions SoftwareCo to retain 80%+ of Classic's customer base (prioritizing the 950 long-term users) while accelerating Modern's roadmap, but only if these safeguards confirm feasibility.

**2. Specific Conditions That Must Be Met Before Proceeding**  
Proceeding requires completing the following pre-mitigations within 1-3 months, each directly anchored to a premortem failure mode. These must be validated with measurable outcomes (e.g., via reports to the CFO and CRO) before any sunset announcement, resource reallocation, or migration planning begins. Failure to meet any condition triggers a pivot to Option B (maintenance-level investment) for stability.

- **For Migration Bottleneck from Unresolved Feature Gaps**: Complete a 4-week engineering audit and prototype sprint with a cross-functional team (4-5 engineers, including 2 from Classic maintenance). This must produce a validated risk register confirming the 20% feature gap (workflow customizations and legacy integrations) can be closed in 4 engineer-quarters or less, with prototypes tested on 2-3 enterprise-critical scenarios (e.g., infrastructure dependencies for the 400 high-ARPU customers). Success metric: At least 90% of enterprise features migration-ready, with contingency plans for any extensions (e.g., 1-2 extra quarters budgeted from the $3.5M savings).

- **For Customer Churn Spike from Poorly Managed Transitions**: The CRO must lead a 6-week customer sentiment pilot, surveying 20% of each segment (80 enterprise, 70 mid-market, 40 SMB, 50 long-tail/education from the 950 long-term users) to co-design incentives (e.g., free consulting for enterprise annual contracts, 6-month Modern trials for SMBs). This must yield a retention playbook with an 80% opt-in rate and trained support protocols to manage the 1.8x ticket disparity. Success metric: Projected churn below 20% across segments, with segment-specific 18-month phasing (enterprise first) capping revenue loss at under $1M.

- **For Brand and Community Backlash from Long-Tail Cutoff**: Form a developer and academic advisory council (10-15 members from the 250 long-tail/education users) for 8 weeks of sessions to develop low-cost Modern tiers (e.g., free basic access for educators) and a "legacy commitment" (e.g., open-sourcing non-core components). Monitor sentiment via social listening on GitHub/Reddit, aiming for 70% positive endorsements. Success metric: A playbook isolating low-ARPU fallout (budgeted at $200K from savings) that converts 60%+ of the segment into Modern advocates, preventing viral backlash.

- **For Internal Silo and Resource Misallocation**: Establish a 4-week cross-functional war room (CFO, CRO, engineering/support leads) to create a shared Gantt chart integrating the 18-month timeline with Modern's roadmap, simulating reallocation (30% capacity boost) and defining joint KPIs (e.g., ticket resolution). Success metric: Unified governance plan ring-fencing 20% of reallocated engineering time for Modern innovations, with documented hidden costs (e.g., under $500K for hires) and no projected dip below 20% YoY growth for Modern.

**3. If Deferring (Not Applicable Here)**  
N/A—We are recommending conditional proceed, as the mitigations provide a clear path to de-risk without indefinite delay. However, if any condition above cannot be met within 3 months (e.g., engineering audit reveals 6+ quarters needed for feature parity), defer to Option B and reassess in 6 months once Modern's growth stabilizes above 25% YoY or CRO data shows <10% projected churn risk.

**4. Acknowledgment of Unmitigated Concerns**  
While the pre-mitigations target the premortem's core failure modes, they do not fully address several adversary and second-order concerns. Competitor exploitation remains a vulnerability: rivals could still poach 20-30% of the 400 enterprise customers during the 18-month phase by offering superior legacy integration tools, especially if economic stress triggers budget scrutiny and early contract terminations. Internal sabotage risks from the 6 reallocated engineers (e.g., knowledge transfer delays or quiet quitting) are partially covered by the war room but not eliminated, potentially extending timelines under talent shortages. Broader second-order effects, like ecosystem isolation from negative analyst coverage spilling to Modern's partnerships or a developer talent exodus amplifying morale issues, are contained but not eradicated—e.g., even with council buy-in, 20-30% of long-tail influencers could still fuel community backlash if incentives underdeliver. These could undermine the $3.5M savings and 25% growth trajectory if external pressures (e.g., downturns) compound during execution, warranting ongoing monitoring post-commitment.
