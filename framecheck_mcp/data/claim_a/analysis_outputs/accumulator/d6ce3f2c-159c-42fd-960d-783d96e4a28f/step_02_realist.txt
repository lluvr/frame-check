Realistically, a sunset would retain 70-80% of Classic's 1,200 customers in Modern, capturing $6-7M of the $9M revenue but at the cost of short-term disruptions, stabilizing total revenue around $60M (Modern's base plus partial Classic migration) rather than the optimist's full $63M+. Mid-market (350 customers) and SMB (200) segments, with moderate-to-low stickiness, could see 15-25% attrition from workflow disruptions, offset partially by upsells but not fully reversing the 8% decline trajectory. The 250 long-tail/education users pose the biggest risk: only 40-50% might convert to low-cost Modern tiers, with the rest amplifying brand damage through community backlash, drawing negative analyst scrutiny on SoftwareCo's loyalty to 8-10 year veterans. Engineering redirection would boost Modern's roadmap modestly (e.g., closing gaps on time but diverting from new features), while costs drop below $3.5M post-sunset—but expect 6-12 months of elevated migration support expenses, netting a leaner but volatile operation where growth dips to 15-20% YoY amid transition friction.
