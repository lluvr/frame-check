#### Pre-Mitigations for Identified Failure Modes
To avert the disaster scenario outlined above, the following pre-mitigations target each failure mode before committing to Option A (sunsetting Product Classic). These are operational actions, designed to be executed in the 1-3 months leading up to any announcement or resource reallocation, drawing on the case's specifics like customer segments, engineering loads, and unaddressed CRO input. Each reduces probability by gathering data and building safeguards, or impact by containing fallout, while aligning with the CFO's margin-recovery goals without derailing the decision.

(a) **Pre-Mitigation for Migration Bottleneck from Unresolved Feature Gaps**: Conduct a targeted engineering audit and prototype sprint to validate the 4 engineer-quarters estimate for closing the 20% feature gap. Operationally, assemble a cross-functional team of 4-5 engineers (including 2 from the 6 Classic maintainers and 2 from Modern) for a 4-week proof-of-concept phase: select 2-3 high-risk legacy integrations and workflow customizations from the 400 enterprise customers' profiles (e.g., via anonymized usage data), build minimal viable ports to Modern, and test them in a sandbox environment with simulated infrastructure dependencies. This generates a detailed risk register with revised timelines (e.g., flagging if certain ports need external API work) and a phased rollout plan, reducing probability of delays by 50% through early detection and allowing contingency budgeting for 1-2 extra engineer-quarters. Impact is contained by prioritizing enterprise-critical features first, ensuring at least 90% of high-ARPU revenue is migration-ready before the 18-month phase begins.

(b) **Pre-Mitigation for Customer Churn Spike from Poorly Managed Transitions**: Engage the CRO immediately for a customer sentiment pilot with the 950 long-term users, focusing on personalized migration readiness. Operationally, the CRO leads a 6-week outreach program: survey 20% of each segment (e.g., 80 enterprise, 70 mid-market, 40 SMB, and 50 long-tail/education customers) via targeted calls or workshops to map pain points (e.g., workflow dependencies), then co-design segment-specific incentives like free migration consulting for enterprise annual contract holders or 6-month Modern trials for SMBs. This produces a retention playbook with success metrics (e.g., 80% opt-in rate) and trains support staff on transition protocols to handle the 1.8x ticket disparity, cutting churn probability by proactively addressing resistance and building buy-in. Impact is limited by segmenting the 18-month phase (e.g., enterprise first, SMB last), capping potential $4M revenue loss at under $1M through data-driven incentives that preserve relationships without inflating the $3.5M operational costs.

(c) **Pre-Mitigation for Brand and Community Backlash from Long-Tail Cutoff**: Launch a developer and academic advisory council to co-create alternatives for the 250 long-tail/education users, mitigating social influence risks. Operationally, in the first 8 weeks, recruit 10-15 influential members from this segment (e.g., via GitHub outreach or academic partnerships) for bi-weekly virtual sessions to brainstorm low-cost Modern access tiers (e.g., free basic features for educators with community feedback loops). Publicize a "legacy legacy" commitment, like open-sourcing non-core Classic components or offering perpetual read-only access, and monitor sentiment through social listening tools on Reddit and GitHub. This reduces backlash probability by converting potential critics into advocates (aiming for 70% positive endorsements), while containing impact by isolating the segment's low ARPU fallout—e.g., allocating $200K from the $3.5M savings for these tiers—ensuring analyst coverage highlights SoftwareCo's community focus rather than disloyalty, and stabilizing internal morale to retain all 6 reallocated engineers.

(d) **Pre-Mitigation for Internal Silo and Resource Misallocation**: Implement a 4-week cross-functional war room to align stakeholders on migration resourcing, bridging the CFO's framing with the CRO's relational view. Operationally, convene weekly sessions with the CFO, CRO, engineering leads (covering the 22 total, including the 6 Classic engineers), and support heads to map resource flows: create a shared Gantt chart integrating the 18-month timeline with Modern's roadmap, simulate reallocation scenarios (e.g., 30% capacity boost vs. migration demands), and define escalation protocols for silos (e.g., joint KPIs like ticket resolution time). This uncovers hidden costs (e.g., $500K for interim hires) and fosters buy-in, slashing probability of delays by establishing unified governance. Impact is mitigated by ring-fencing 20% of the reallocated engineering time for Modern innovations, ensuring YoY growth doesn't dip below 20% and preserving the $3.5M savings net of any upfront planning investments, while preempting board scrutiny through documented alignment.
