### Strongest Reasons to Proceed with This Decision
From an optimist frame, proceeding with sunsetting Product Classic (Option A) is strongly supported by the financial and operational efficiencies already evident in the case. The product's $9M annual revenue comes at a $3.5M fully-loaded cost, yielding a healthy margin that could improve dramatically post-sunset, aligning with the CFO's margin-recovery view but extending it to fuel broader growth. Redirecting the 6 full-time engineers (out of 22 total) would unlock 30% more capacity for Product Modern, which is already driving 70% of revenue with 25% YoY growth—far outpacing Classic's 8% decline. This reallocation could accelerate Modern's roadmap, such as enhancing the 80% feature overlap to full parity in just 4 engineer-quarters, minimizing disruption. Customer support's 1.8x higher ticket rate for Classic indicates it's a drag on efficiency; sunsetting would streamline operations, freeing resources to serve Modern's growing base more effectively. With 950 long-term customers (5+ years) mostly in high-stickiness enterprise and mid-market segments (750 total), a phased 18-month migration leverages their annual contracts and dependencies to ensure smooth transitions, turning potential resistance into loyalty reinforcement.

Engineering gains would supercharge innovation, delivering the 4 engineer-quarters of work to achieve full feature parity ahead of schedule, while the 30% capacity boost enables new Modern enhancements that delight all segments.
