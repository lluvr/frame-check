### Step 4 - PREMORTEM (Continued: Pre-Mitigation Proposals)

Building on the premortem's four failure modes, this section proposes targeted pre-mitigations for each. These are proactive steps to implement *before* final commitment to either hire (Sarah or Marcus), designed to reduce the probability of the failure mode occurring or blunt its impact if it does. Each pre-mitigation is operationalized with specific actions, timelines, responsible parties, and success criteria, ensuring they fit within BuildCo's Series B constraints (e.g., minimal added cost, leveraging existing resources like the CEO and HR). The goal is to de-risk the decision without delaying momentum, such as by integrating these into the offer negotiation or promotion process over the next 2-4 weeks.

(a) **Pre-Mitigation for Sarah's Cultural Misfit Igniting Team Revolt and Knowledge Drain**: To preempt cultural friction, conduct a structured "shadow integration" trial before extending a formal offer. Operationally, this involves inviting Sarah to two half-day virtual sessions (one with the CEO and one with 3-4 key team members) focused on collaborative problem-solving exercises, such as co-brainstorming a hypothetical ABM campaign for BuildCo's niche using anonymized internal data. Schedule these within 1-2 weeks of initial interviews, facilitated by HR or an external neutral coach (budget: $5K max for facilitation). Include anonymous post-session feedback surveys rating her "readability" and collaboration on a 1-5 scale, plus a team debrief led by the CEO to gauge fit intuitively. Success criteria: Average feedback score >4/5 and no more than 1 team member flagging concerns; if below threshold, pivot to Marcus or refine the offer with a 90-day probation clause tied to retention KPIs. This reduces probability by surfacing misfit early (before the $80K buyout) and limits impact by building buy-in, potentially cutting revolt risk by 50% through demonstrated adaptation.

(b) **Pre-Mitigation for Marcus's Promotion Revealing Scalability Blind Spots, Stifling Innovation**: To test Marcus's VP readiness without full commitment, initiate a "scalability simulation" assignment during the decision window. Operationally, task Marcus with leading a 2-week internal project to design and pitch a scaled marketing plan (e.g., expanding the team to 10 with budget for ABM pilots targeting a new enterprise vertical), incorporating external benchmarks like competitor case studies provided by the CEO. He presents to a small advisory group (CEO, CRO, and one board observer) at week 3, with structured evaluation on criteria like strategic breadth, innovation (e.g., integrating AI tools), and execution feasibility, scored via a rubric (e.g., 70% threshold for "VP-ready"). HR documents this as a low-stakes development exercise to avoid pressure. Success criteria: Meets 80% of rubric elements, with feedback highlighting growth areas; if gaps emerge, commit to the external coach from day 1 of promotion. This de-risks by revealing blind spots pre-promotion (no cost beyond internal time) and mitigates impact via built-in support, potentially halving the chance of stagnation by accelerating his ramp-up.

(c) **Pre-Mitigation for Non-Compete and Timeline Delays Cascading into Momentum-Killing Gaps**: To neutralize legal and timing risks, perform comprehensive "non-compete stress-testing" via third-party legal review before any buyout negotiation. Operationally, engage BuildCo's external counsel (or a SaaS-specialized firm like Cooley, budgeted at $10-15K) to conduct a 10-day audit of Sarah's contract, including scenario modeling for disputes (e.g., IP claims on GTM playbooks) and drafting a phased buyout agreement with milestones (e.g., $40K upfront, $40K post-start, with arbitration clauses). Simultaneously, prepare a "bridge plan" with Marcus: Assign him interim VP duties for 4-6 weeks (with 10% comp bump as incentive), augmented by a $20K fractional consultant for any enterprise gaps. Test the plan via a dry-run simulation of a Q3 campaign handoff. Success criteria: Legal review confirms <20% dispute risk and clear path to 6-week start; bridge plan validated by CEO walkthrough showing no pipeline dip. If risks exceed thresholds, abandon Sarah and promote Marcus immediately. This lowers probability through due diligence (avoiding surprise escalations) and caps impact by ensuring continuity, potentially saving $100K+ in fees and 2 months of delay.

(d) **Pre-Mitigation for Either Choice Failing to Free CEO Bandwidth, Triggering Executive Burnout and Fractures**: To enforce bandwidth reduction from the outset, establish a "handoff protocol blueprint" co-developed with the chosen candidate pre-commitment. Operationally, during final interviews or promotion discussions (within 1 week), collaboratively outline a 6-month transition roadmap: Week 1-4 (daily 30-min check-ins, CEO delegates 50% of tasks); Month 2-3 (weekly meetings, CEO at 15% time); Month 4-6 (monthly reviews, CEO <10%). Include measurable KPIs like "CEO marketing hours logged via shared calendar" and escalation triggers (e.g., if >20% at month 3, activate third-party mediation like an executive coach at $15K). The CEO and candidate sign off on this as a binding addendum to the offer/promotion letter, with HR monitoring via bi-weekly pulse checks. Success criteria: 50% bandwidth drop by month 1 and sustained <15% by month 3, per logged data; adjust based on interim reviews. This prevents overload by embedding accountability (zero added cost beyond documentation) and reduces burnout risk by 60% through structured delegation, while enabling early pivots if fractures appear in cross-functional ties (e.g., via CRO input in reviews).
