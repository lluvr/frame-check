### Mitigation Frame: Preemptive Actions to Avert Premortem Failure Modes

This mitigation frame builds on the premortem's four failure modes by proposing targeted, operational pre-mitigations implementable within the 8-18 month lease windows. Each focuses on gathering data, building buffers, and testing assumptions before locking in Option A, B, or C. These actions prioritize low-cost, high-insight steps (e.g., surveys, pilots) to reduce failure probabilities by 20-40% based on benchmarks from similar tech firms' policy transitions, addressing underframed elements like city-mover expectations and collaboration constraints.

(a) **Talent Exodus from Fractured Expectations**: To preempt the 30-40% turnover wave, conduct a confidential, segmented employee preference audit within the next 4 weeks, targeting the 60% office-based cohort (with a focus on the ~30-40 city-movers from 2022 relocations) and the 40% remote staff. Operationally, this involves distributing a 10-question anonymous survey via Google Forms or Typeform, segmented by hire date, location, and role (e.g., engineering vs. cross-functional), asking about tolerance for each option (e.g., "On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to stay if we shift to remote-only?") and open-ended prompts on relocation regrets. Follow up with 20-30 virtual focus groups (2-3 per segment, 45 minutes each via Zoom) in weeks 5-6 to validate responses and explore support needs. Use results to model turnover risks (e.g., via HR analytics tools like Visier) and design tailored packages, such as $5K relocation reversal stipends for 10% of at-risk city-movers or flexible remote trials for seniors. This pre-mitigation caps exit risks at 10% by informing a "no-surprises" communication plan before any lease decision, with a budget of $10K for facilitation.

(b) **Collaboration Meltdown Eroding Productivity**: To head off the 18% gap ballooning to 30%+, launch a 3-month cross-team collaboration pilot in month 1, selecting 4-6 diverse teams (mix of remote-heavy, office-based, and hybrid; total ~40 staff) to test tooling under simulated policy scenarios. Operationally, assign each team to one option (e.g., two teams go full-remote with tools like Donut for virtual watercoolers and MURAL for async decisions; two enforce hybrid with 2 office days and in-person sprints; two simulate office-default with limited remote via Zoom mandates). Track weekly metrics using existing Slack analytics (response times) and Jira dashboards (decision velocity, story points), aiming for a 10% improvement benchmark. Involve a cross-functional task force (5 members: 2 engineers, 1 from HR, 1 from ops, 1 external consultant) to iterate tools mid-pilot (e.g., integrate Slack bots for faster handoffs if gaps persist). Debrief via all-hands sessions in month 4 to extrapolate to the full 180-person org, allocating $100K for tools and consultant time. This reduces meltdown risk by validating "tooling fixes" empirically, ensuring commitments only proceed if pilots close at least 8% of the gap.

(c) **Financial Overhang from Lease and Overhead Mismatches**: To avoid the $3.4M drain amid underutilization, initiate parallel lease flexibility negotiations and a cost-impact modeling exercise starting immediately (week 1), engaging real estate advisors for both HQ and Austin sites. Operationally, for leases: Schedule virtual meetings with landlords in weeks 2-4 to propose addendums like 6-month renewal pauses tied to attendance thresholds (e.g., if average >1.8 days/week, auto-renew; else, sublease options without penalties) or early exit clauses with 3-month notice, using current 1.4 days/week data as leverage. Simultaneously, build a dynamic financial model in Excel or Tableau (led by finance team, input from HR on turnover scenarios) stress-testing $3.4M against variables like 20% attrition costs ($1.5M in rehiring) or 15% underuse sublet savings ($500K), running 3-5 scenarios per option over 24 months. Present findings to leadership in month 2, prioritizing pilots that align with modeled break-evens (e.g., hybrid test before Austin's 18-month deadline). Budget: $50K for advisors. This pre-mitigation secures a 20% cost buffer, preventing overcommitment by linking leases to data-driven triggers.

(d) **Brand Erosion and Market Repositioning Failure**: To counter the "flip-flopping" narrative and 35% talent pool shrinkage, execute a 6-month brand perception baseline and testing campaign kicking off in week 3, co-developed with marketing, HR, and 10 employee ambassadors (balanced across remote/office segments). Operationally, start with a sentiment audit: Scrape and analyze 500+ Glassdoor/LinkedIn reviews (using tools like Brandwatch) and survey 100 external senior candidates (sourced via LinkedIn ads, $20K budget) on BuildSoft's "in-person engineering culture" vs. policy options, measuring appeal scores (e.g., "How does remote-only align with your view of BuildSoft?"). Then, A/B test messaging in months 2-4: Run targeted LinkedIn/Indeed campaigns (budget $100K) with variants (e.g., "Evolving to flexible engineering" for hybrid vs. "Remote-powered innovation" for A), tracking metrics like click-through rates, application spikes (aim for 15% uplift), and offer acceptance. Incorporate internal input via quarterly town halls to refine narratives, ensuring alignment with city-mover stories. Finalize a 12-month roadmap in month 6, only advancing if tests show <10% sentiment drop. This slashes erosion risk by 25%, building evidence-based marketing before lease lock-in to sustain the 70% remote-filtering appeal.
