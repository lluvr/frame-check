### Structured Commitment Statement for BuildSoft Policy Renewal Decision

**1. Recommended Commitment: Proceed with Conditions**

Proceed with Option B (Hybrid Maintenance), committing to renew the HQ lease in 8 months and Austin lease in 18 months at the projected $3.4M annual cost, while enforcing the current 2-day-per-week office expectation with targeted incentives (e.g., stipends for in-person days). This option best balances the 12% engineering productivity edge, the 65% remote preference among recent hires, and the 18% collaboration deficit, leveraging the 8-18 month lease window for testing without the high disruption risks of Option A (remote-only) or C (office-default). Full implementation is conditioned on the successful completion of the pre-mitigation actions outlined in Step 5, which directly address the premortem's four failure modes. These conditions must be met within 6 months to validate assumptions and cap risks before lease lock-in.

**2. Specific Conditions for Proceeding**

The following conditions are anchored to the premortem failure modes (a-d) and draw directly from the operational pre-mitigations in Step 5. Each must yield empirical evidence of risk reduction (e.g., via defined benchmarks) before advancing to lease renewal and policy enforcement. If any condition fails its benchmark, revert to deferral.

- **Condition for Failure Mode (a) - Talent Exodus from Fractured Expectations**: Complete the 4-week employee preference audit (anonymous survey + 20-30 focus groups segmented by office/remote cohorts and 2022 city-movers) by month 2, modeling turnover risks to show projected voluntary exits below 15% across all staff segments under Option B. If met, implement tailored support packages (e.g., $5K relocation reversals for 10% of at-risk city-movers) and a communication plan to secure internal buy-in; if not, cap enforcement incentives at 1.5 days/week to minimize alienation of the 60% office-based staff.

- **Condition for Failure Mode (b) - Collaboration Meltdown Eroding Productivity**: Execute the 3-month cross-team collaboration pilot (4-6 teams testing hybrid scenarios with tools like Slack bots and MURAL) by month 4, achieving at least an 8% improvement in collaboration metrics (e.g., Slack response times and decision velocity) for hybrid-enforced teams, while maintaining the 12% productivity premium in story points. If met, allocate $100K for full rollout of validated tools; if not, supplement with mandatory virtual training to address the 18% gap before scaling to the 180-person organization.

- **Condition for Failure Mode (c) - Financial Overhang from Lease and Overhead Mismatches**: Finalize lease negotiations and financial modeling by month 2, securing flexible clauses (e.g., 6-month renewal pauses tied to >1.8 days/week attendance) for both HQ and Austin sites, with stress-tested scenarios showing Option B's $3.4M cost offset by at least 10% efficiency gains (e.g., from reduced turnover or sublet savings) under 20% attrition assumptions. If met, proceed with renewals only if models confirm a 20% cost buffer; if not, prioritize subleasing 20% of space to mitigate underutilization risks from the current 1.4 days/week trend.

- **Condition for Failure Mode (d) - Brand Erosion and Market Repositioning Failure**: Deliver the 6-month brand perception audit and A/B testing campaign by month 6, demonstrating a 15% uplift in senior candidate appeal (e.g., application rates and offer acceptance for hybrid messaging) among the 70% remote-filtering pool, with no more than a 10% drop in sentiment on Glassdoor/LinkedIn tied to "in-person engineering culture" evolution. If met, approve the 12-month brand roadmap with employee input; if not, pause marketing pushes and refine narratives to better address the 25% hybrid-preferring hires.

These conditions will be tracked via a cross-functional oversight team (HR, engineering, finance, marketing leads) with monthly progress reports to leadership, ensuring data-driven progression within the lease timelines.

**3. Deferral Triggers (If Conditions Are Not Met)**

If any condition fails its benchmark by month 6, defer full commitment to Option B and extend the hybrid status quo without lease renewals (e.g., pursue short-term sublets or extensions). Proceeding would unlock only upon: (i) updated employee audits showing <10% turnover risk post-mitigation adjustments; (ii) pilot data confirming 10%+ collaboration gains with sustained productivity; (iii) revised lease terms providing 30%+ flexibility (e.g., no-penalty exits); or (iv) external talent market shifts, such as a 20% drop in remote-filtering rates among seniors or competitor policy reversals, validated via quarterly benchmarking. Deferral would last 6-12 months to allow iterative testing, avoiding the $3.4M lock-in amid unresolved uncertainties.

**4. Unmitigated Concerns Acknowledgment**

This commitment does not fully mitigate several adversary and premortem concerns, particularly those tied to second-order effects and external hostilities. Specifically: (i) competitor poaching could still exploit policy uncertainty during the 6-month conditioning period, potentially inflating the 40% senior offer loss rate to 50%+ via targeted campaigns, as mitigations focus on internal audits but lack direct countermeasures like counter-recruitment incentives; (ii) the risk of internal factions (e.g., resentment between 60% office-based and 40% remote staff) forming "quiet quitting" patterns or IP leaks remains partially unaddressed, as pilots and surveys provide snapshots but cannot guarantee long-term cohesion amid economic stress; (iii) brand dilution from historical "in-person engineering culture" whiplash could persist beyond testing, with ex-employee reviews amplifying perceptions of flip-flopping and deterring partnerships, since marketing A/B tests measure candidate appeal but not broader ecosystem trust; and (iv) catastrophic financial cascades (e.g., $1M+ subleasing disputes or investor scrutiny during downturns) are buffered but not eliminated, as lease negotiations depend on landlord cooperation, which premortem scenarios highlight as a potential tripwire. These gaps underscore the need for ongoing monitoring, with a 20% probability of partial failure even if conditions are met.
