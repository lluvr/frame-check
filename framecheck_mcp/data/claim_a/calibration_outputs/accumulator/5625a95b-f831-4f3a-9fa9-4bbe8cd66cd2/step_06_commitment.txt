### Structured Commitment for ScienceLab's Investment Decision

#### 1. Recommended Commitment: Proceed with Conditions
Accept the $5M investment from BCSV at $30M post-money valuation, provided all specified conditions below are met within the 14-day close window. This recommendation balances the Optimist's upside of runway extension (to 23 months), strategic channel access for ARR acceleration, and premium valuation against the Realist's and Adversary's risks of covenant-induced lock-in, fundraising friction, and dependency on BigCorp's execution. Proceeding conditionally leverages the 14-day pressure as negotiation leverage, using tier-2 sheets (Sequence and Threshold) as a credible backstop to force covenant softening.

#### 2. Specific Conditions for Proceeding
These conditions directly address the Premortem's four failure modes by operationalizing Step 5's mitigations. They must be secured in writing via term sheet amendments or side letters, verified by startup counsel (budget $20-50K from reserves), and confirmed through diligence (e.g., 3-5 calls with BigCorp stakeholders) before signing. Failure to meet any condition triggers walking to the tier-2 path (e.g., closing Sequence's $4M sheet for 4 months of runway while pursuing organic $2M ARR).

- **Condition for Failure Mode (a) - Channel Access Fizzles into Strategic Dependency**: Secure a non-binding LOI from BigCorp committing to at least two joint go-to-market pilots with named Big5 accounts (e.g., 10-15 ICP-overlapping enterprises) within 60 days post-close, with enforceable KPIs (e.g., 5 qualified leads per quarter, $2M ARR target in 12 months). Include an off-ramp clause allowing partial refund of $1M if KPIs miss by month 6. This is validated via a 48-hour "channel diligence sprint" (sales lead + technical expert conducting calls with BigCorp's enterprise directors to map historical success rates and ICP overlap), ensuring the "immediate Big5 access" is not aspirational.

- **Condition for Failure Mode (b) - ROFR Triggers Fundraising Paralysis and Valuation Collapse**: Amend the ROFR to include a "tier-1 carve-out" limiting its scope to non-competitive financings (e.g., excluding rounds led by the two named tier-1 funds or VCs >$500M AUM), with a strict 30-day exercise window and automatic waiver if BigCorp declines to match within 10% of lead terms. If unamendable, restructure as a convertible note ($5M at 20% discount to $30M cap) to bypass ROFR. Leverage tier-2 sheets in negotiations and survey the tier-1 funds pre-close for confirmation that the carve-out restores their interest at $2M ARR.

- **Condition for Failure Mode (c) - Exclusivity Blocks Pivotal Partnerships Amid Competitive Surge**: Shorten the 18-month exclusivity to 12 months and add narrow carve-outs for non-competitive alliances with the three named competitors (e.g., co-marketing or integrations <20% overlapping with BigCorp's ICP, verified by shared client lists). Present an internal "partnership pipeline map" (detailing 5-10 potential deals worth $2-5M ARR each) to BCSV as negotiation ammo, and secure a side letter from BigCorp guaranteeing equivalent revenue replacement (e.g., $3M in co-sell commitments) if carve-outs are limited. Conduct a pre-close audit of competitor deals via advisors to quantify and reference blocked value.

- **Condition for Failure Mode (d) - Observer and Metrics Enable Internal Sabotage and Cultural Decay**: Limit the board observer to quarterly meetings only (no ad-hoc access or veto power) and scope quarterly metrics to high-level aggregates (e.g., ARR/churn totals, excluding IP roadmaps, customer data, hires, or prototypes). Implement a data room protocol with NDAs for all shares and an escalation clause converting the observer to zero access if team attrition exceeds 15% (monitored via monthly anonymous surveys). Run an internal governance workshop pre-close to simulate and flag risks, using BCSV's urgency to enforce these limits.

Closing on these conditions extends runway to 23 months with reduced covenant risks, enabling focus on 18% MoM growth and Big5 pilots while retaining 70-80% optionality for tier-1 rounds at $2M ARR.

- **Macro/Regulatory Shocks (Adversary Market Vectors)**: The 14-day pressure limits deep legal review of antitrust exposure (e.g., FTC scrutiny of exclusivity as bundling), leaving ~15% risk of $2-5M compliance costs or forced unwinds if AI enterprise probes intensify, unhedged by tier-2's cleaner path.
These residual risks mean the deal remains a calculated bet on BigCorp's goodwill, with potential for 20-30% valuation drag if channel upside underperforms—warranting ongoing monitoring (e.g., quarterly internal reviews) and a tier-2 bridge as Plan B.
