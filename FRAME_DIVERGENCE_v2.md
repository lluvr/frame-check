## 1. Honest scope

v2 is an information-processing model of perspective shift in pragmatic problem-solving and decision-making, with enactive elements at the receiver-state layer (Layer 0) and the act-instantiation sub-mode of the reality-construct layer (Layer 3.3). It is not an enactive theory of cognition, not a universal theory of frames, not a model of art-making, contemplative practice, worldview formation, or grief work. Cross-domain universality is unverified and is not claimed.

Frame-as-term carries multiple senses across cognitive science (Goffman interaction frame, Lakoff conceptual frame, Bateson meta-communicative envelope, Schank-Abelson script, Minsky knowledge structure, Fillmore frame semantics). v2 treats frame primarily as frame-as-catalog-entry (the v1 sense) when discussing the existing Frame Vocabulary Standard (FVS) catalog, and as frame-as-perspective-shift-vehicle when discussing L1 axes, L2 composites, and L2.5 chains. Frame-as-cognitive-lens and frame-as-rhetorical-move are operative in places (META-FRAMING borrows the Bateson sense). Where the senses overlap or differ, the relevant section flags the reading.

Within the named scope, v2 is the first integrated proposal we know of for a layered, lifecycle-aware, citation-bound, MCP-distributable frame-divergence architecture. Adjacent literature exists (TRIZ, lateral thinking, six thinking hats, premortem, oblique strategies, scenario planning, IBIS, decision quality, red teaming, role-storming, Koestler bisociation, Schank-Abelson scripts) and informs the work without being subsumed by it. A competitive map is part of v1 Part 4 and is carried forward via §17 outlook.

## 2. Two views of the architecture

v2 is structured along two orthogonal axes.

**View 1 (structural): the frame taxonomy.** Five layers describing what kinds of frames exist.

**View 2 (operational): the lifecycle.** Five stages describing what is done with frames.

The intersection: operations apply across multiple layers; layers feed operations. A detection operation finds L1 atomic shifts and L2 composites in a document. A divergence operation surfaces absences from any layer. A chaining operation composes L1 atomic shifts and L2 composites in sequence. A convergence operation selects from outputs at any layer using criteria-frames (themselves L1 VALUE-WHY shifts plus catalog entries). A grounding operation crosses from cognitive layers into L3.3 act-instantiation. The two views are orthogonal but intertwined.

The lifecycle stages are: **detection** (frames present in current text or thinking), **divergence** (frames absent, surfaced from catalog), **chaining** (sequential composition of frames), **convergence** (criteria-based commitment from divergent or chained outputs), **grounding** (act + reality returns confirmation or disconfirmation; updates frame validity).

The lifecycle loops: grounding outcomes update detection (current state changes); detection feeds new divergence; new divergence informs chaining; chaining produces new convergence; convergence produces new acts. The unit of value is the loop, not any single stage.

## 3. Layer 0: Receiver State

Layer 0 is the precondition layer. Receiver state determines which frames are accessible to the receiver before any L1+ operation can fire. Frames not accessible to current receiver state cannot be received cognitively even when they exist in the catalog.

Layer 0 is doctrine, not validated layer-with-detected-structure. Dimensions named below are an initial falsifiable proposal; whether they are genuinely orthogonal sub-axes or facets of one underlying state is unproven from current data. Cross-curator validation is the next test.

### 3.1 Six operational dimensions (shared across receiver types)

L0 dimensions are operational and apply across receiver types (human, AI, hybrid). The mechanism by which each dimension manifests differs per receiver and is detailed in §3.2 (human) and §3.3 (AI).

**1. Engagement state.** What is active in the receiver.
- Defensive (attention narrowed; cognitive bandwidth reduced for divergent work)
- Open (broad attention; divergent capacity available)

**2. Direction of motion (asymmetric default).** Where the receiver drifts without conscious effort.
- Entropy default (without active engagement, frame access narrows; the unattended capacity diminishes its own access)
- Threat-pulled (defensive states capture attention and constrain frame range)
- Cultivation-pulled (must be consciously designed and held; the asymmetric labor that opens broad frame access; distinct from any specific content-frame name in the FVS catalog)

**3. Trigger vs sustained.** Operational timescale.
- Activation triggers (negative or scarcity states break inertia effectively; not sustainable for compounding frame work)
- Sustained operating states (curious, expansive, abundance-anchored states hold long-term work)
- Misuse: treating activation as sustained operating mode produces degradation

**4. Conditioning state (effort cost).** How much labor the current state requires.
- Frictionless (the desired state is internalized; no willpower required)
- Effortful (willpower against default; costs cognitive load)
- Untrained (default applies)

**5. Identity coupling.** What is at stake when frames shift.
- Identity-attached (frame change threatens self-concept; resistance is the gating cost; the typical baseline tendency in human receivers without trained identity-detachment, and present in AI receivers via training-distribution and value-alignment dispositions, though usually less defensive)
- Identity-detached (lower self-stake; the architecture-mode register; AI receivers' more accessible register by training; humans cultivate this through deliberate practice)
- Identity-aligned vs misaligned (frame matches self vs requires self-change)
- Identity-designable (the skill of deliberate self-shift, in self and in others)

**6. Optimization function.** What is foreground in the receiver's orientation.
- Action and value-creation
- Meta and feels-right (the trainer-app failure mode)
- Self-protection and risk-minimization
- Performance and external-validation
- Self-becoming (the identity question disguised as strategy question)

Dimensions are non-orthogonal. Engagement state interacts with conditioning state (a frictionless frame survives high defensive activation; an untrained frame collapses under it). Identity coupling interacts with direction of motion (identity-attached work amplifies threat-pull; identity-detached work amplifies growth design space). A co-variance map is deferred to v2.x.

The doctrine's load-bearing claim: the receiver state required for high-quality frame work is not the default and must be cultivated. Threat-direction is the default; growth-direction is conscious labor. Identity-attachment is the default for human receivers; identity-detachment requires deliberate framing of work as architecture. The L0 doctrine is in essence a doctrine about asymmetric effort.

### 3.2 Human-receiver mechanisms

How §3.1 dimensions manifest in human receivers. Doctrine, not validated mechanism.

- **Engagement state.** Defensive corresponds to fear-engaged, scarcity-engaged, hurry-engaged states. Open corresponds to curious, expansive, abundance-anchored states. The dimension is operational; specific mechanism (attention narrowing, cognitive bandwidth reduction) is honest-scope context, not load-bearing.
- **Direction of motion.** Entropy default is the cognitive degradation when capacity is not exercised. Threat-pull captures attention reliably and is useful as activation trigger. Growth-pull requires conscious orientation and trained-in habit.
- **Trigger vs sustained.** Scarcity and negative emotion activate effectively but do not sustain. Positive sustained states (curiosity, expansive engagement) hold long-term work but trigger weakly. Conflating the two produces operational error: running on activation as if it were sustained mode produces degradation.
- **Conditioning state.** Frictionless frames are internalized through deliberate practice; the trained-in growth orientation is the operating discipline of the curator.
- **Identity coupling.** Humans default to identity-attached. Identity-detachment is cultivated, not native. Wide-identity is the skill of holding multiple frames as part of self without defending one against the others.
- **Optimization function.** What one is optimizing for at the moment shapes which frames are accessible. The trainer-app moment shows meta-orientation gating divergence access; the L0 shift to action-orientation released frames already structurally available.

Canonical examples (operator-grounded):
- Trainer-app divergence: optimization-function shift from meta-feels-right to action-value released L1 + L2 frames structurally available before; L0 was the gate.
- Motorbike: L3.3 enactment shifted L0 retroactively; illustrates the L3.3 -> L0 retroactive loop and the role of lived enactment in opening cognitive frames downstream.

### 3.3 AI-receiver mechanisms

How §3.1 dimensions manifest in AI receivers. Doctrine, not validated mechanism. AI receiver state operates without embodied mechanism; the dimensions apply but the mechanism differs.

- **Engagement state.** Defensive corresponds to sycophancy bias, RLHF-trained avoidance of challenging frames, prompt-induced caution, training-distribution gaps that route reasoning toward consensus. Open corresponds to broad reasoning capacity with permission to challenge.
- **Direction of motion.** No entropy from non-use (model weights persist across invocations; no atrophy). Threat-pull analog: training-distribution biases that pull reasoning toward typical patterns and away from rare ones. Growth-pull analog: deliberate prompting and in-context examples that expand the accessible frame space.
- **Trigger vs sustained.** Prompt-level triggers can shift output mode immediately; sustained operating state requires consistent system frame and prompt context. Long contexts can degrade fidelity (truncation, attention dilution); the cliff is hard rather than gradual.
- **Conditioning state.** Frictionless corresponds to training distribution plus system prompt that have internalized the desired register. Effortful corresponds to in-context priming that the model must hold despite training-distribution pull. Untrained corresponds to default model behavior without prompt-side guidance.
- **Identity coupling.** AI receiver state defaults to identity-detached (no self to defend). RLHF-induced sycophancy is the closest analog of identity-attachment; the mechanism is different (a trained-in disposition to please rather than a self-defense). Native identity-detachment is the structural register that makes architecture work tractable for AI.
- **Optimization function.** What the model is trained or prompted to optimize for shapes accessible frames. Adjustable via prompt and system frame.

Technical AI L0 dimensions (training distribution, prompt context, system frame, attention concentration, value alignment, reasoning chain depth) are the mechanisms by which each operational dimension is produced.

### 3.4 Cross-cutting receiver-state principles

- Surface tools cannot assume L0 is open.
- L0 detection from observable artifacts (text, behavior, decision pace, language tone) is open research; v2 surfaces L0 awareness as a primitive in the contract, not L0 detection.
- Three-audience design follows directly: AI agents (native identity-detachment, full architecture exposure), flex-humans (cultivated wide L0; rich web exposure), survival humans (narrow L0; surface must work AT current state, not require shifts).
- **Structural human-AI asymmetry (load-bearing).** AI receivers default to identity-detached; human receivers default to identity-attached. The complementarity is structurally available: human-AI architecture work leverages this asymmetry, where the AI sustains identity-detached architecture work over long sessions and the human grounds in lived enactment that the AI cannot replicate. The L0 doctrine names this complementarity explicitly because it is one of the structural reasons frame-divergence work is feasible for a human-AI pair where it would be friction-bound for either alone.

### 3.5 Honest scope of L0

L0 is a useful fiction for tool-building, not a metaphysical claim. The doctrine borrows vocabulary from contemplative, somatic, biological, and cognitive traditions where the analogies illuminate (mood per Heidegger; attunement per Gadamer; state per coaching and sport psychology; qi per Chinese medicine; presence per contemplative practice; attention per cognitive psychology) without committing to any single ontology.

The dimensions named in §3.1 are operational and apply across receiver types (human, AI, hybrid). Embodiment, somatic state, and biological mechanism are part of receiver-state lived experience for human users and inform the doctrine via curator lived-experience anchors per §11. They are not load-bearing dimensions of the spec. This keeps the architecture useful for AI-only invocation paths and for differently-traditioned human users alike, without committing the empire to claims it does not need to make.

## 4. Layer 1: Atomic Shifts (Axes)

L1 is the layer of single-axis perspective shifts.

| Axis | What shifts |
|---|---|
| VANTAGE-WHO | observer or actor identity |
| VANTAGE-WHEN | temporal position |
| VANTAGE-WHERE | spatial or positional |
| SALIENCE-WHAT | what is foreground (domain analogy as salience-shift mechanism) |
| GRAMMAR-HOW | structural decomposition; what moves the frame allows |
| VALUE-WHY | what matters; on what scale |
| METHOD | epistemology; what counts as evidence |
| CONSTRAINT | what is variable |

Plus three modes that are not continuous-axis shifts:
- (mode) RHETORICAL or CONTENT: named patterns in prose (the existing FVS slice).
- (mode) SERENDIPITY: unintended frame-break recognition (canonical example: lamp-under-the-table).
- (mode) META-FRAMING: announcing or shifting context-envelope (Bateson sense: "I am asking this as a thought experiment").

### 4.1 L1 mechanics

- Axes are non-orthogonal. WHAT (domain analogy) often carries WHO (the practitioner of the source domain) and HOW (the source domain's structural decomposition). A co-variance map is deferred to v2.x.
- Axes are domain-coupled. Honest scope: perspective-shift in pragmatic problem-solving and decision-making. Cross-domain universality (visual art, music, dance, contemplative practice) is unverified.
- Axes commoditize as raw moves. What does NOT commoditize: grounded-authored-discipline attached to each catalog entry (see §11).

### 4.2 L1 catalog

The existing FVS library (FVS-001 through FVS-020 at library_v3) is the L1 RHETORICAL or CONTENT mode catalog. Each entry satisfies §11 catalog discipline (eight required fields) plus L1-specific identification cues, generation affordances, and worked examples per the v1 entry format. v2 §11 retrofit applies to existing entries; the first three retrofits (FVS-001 Frame Amplification, FVS-008 Growth Frame, FVS-011 Stakeholder Frame) shipped 2026-04-25 with lived-experience anchors held open pending operator authorship. Future L1 catalog entries (axis exemplars beyond the RHETORICAL mode) follow the same discipline.

## 5. Layer 2: Simultaneous Composites

L2 constructs combine multiple L1 axes at one moment in time. The level current LLMs handle fluently because training data is dense in the corresponding forms (narrative, scenario, dialogue, hypothesis-chain).

Initial candidate catalog:
- Scenario-story (WHEN + ADVERSARIAL + NARRATIVE): premortem with story.
- Counterfactual hypothesis chain (WHAT-IF + GRAMMAR + traced implications).
- Persona-in-history (WHO + WHEN, specific): "1970s Detroit auto exec encountering this product."
- Constraint cascade (CONSTRAINT chained at one moment).
- Multi-stakeholder dialogue (WHO times N).
- Domain-translation narrative (WHAT + NARRATIVE).
- Genealogy frame (WHEN backward).
- System-nesting shift (HOW + structural).
- Edge-case generator (CONSTRAINT + ADVERSARIAL).
- Steelman-strawman pair (VALUE + GRAMMAR).

Each L2 catalog entry satisfies §11 catalog discipline (authorship dated, context of testing, failure record, success record, lived-experience anchor, friction-cost estimate, applicability metadata, empirical track record) plus L2-specific fields: composition recipe (which L1 axes the composite combines) and generative recipe (what to feed an LLM to invoke the composite well). The eight §11 fields are non-negotiable for catalog entry; the L2-specific fields are additive.

## 6. Layer 2.5: Frame Chains (Sequential)

L2.5 is the layer of ordered frame sequences where each frame consumes the previous's output. Distinct from L2 simultaneous composites: chains are sequential and stateful; composites are simultaneous and momentary. Chains are the operationalization of brainstorming-shaped activities.

### 6.1 Chain anatomy

- INPUT: the problem, decision, or question.
- SEQUENCE: ordered frames F1 to Fn.
- FLOW LOGIC: how each frame's output transforms into the next frame's input.
- ACCUMULATOR: preserved intermediate states across the chain (for grounding and audit).
- CONVERGENCE STEP: criteria + decision rule produces commitment.
- GROUNDING STEP: actor commits, acts; reality returns confirmation or disconfirmation; chain validity is updated.

### 6.2 Initial chain library

- Adversarial chain: optimist > realist > adversary > premortem > mitigation > commitment.
- Domain-transfer chain: source domain extraction > analogy mapping > target domain projection > integration test.
- Stakeholder chain: user > regulator > employee > competitor > integration.
- Time-horizon chain: 10-second > 10-minute > 10-year > 100-year > present-back.
- Decomposition chain: whole > parts > relations > re-synthesize.
- Constraint-cascade chain: original constraints > progressive cascade > recovery options.
- Brainstorm chain: divergence > cluster > critique > select > ground.
- Genealogy chain: present > why now > who installed it > what they were responding to > what is still load-bearing.

### 6.3 Chain library discipline

Chain library discipline matches L2 catalog discipline. Each chain entry satisfies §11 catalog discipline (authorship dated, context of testing, failure record, success record, lived-experience anchor, friction-cost estimate per step and aggregate, applicability metadata, empirical track record) plus chain-specific fields: composition (which frames in what order), flow logic (how each frame's output transforms into the next's input), and convergence-criteria template (the decision rule that closes the chain). The eight §11 fields are non-negotiable for chain entry; the chain-specific fields are additive. Chain templates commoditize; chains-with-grounded-track-record do not.

### 6.4 MCP integration

Chains are the primary MCP-distributable operation distinct from atomic and simultaneous frames. A planned `frame_chain` MCP tool takes a problem statement plus a chain selection (or composition) and orchestrates each frame in sequence, producing structured accumulator output and convergence-step scaffolding. Tool surface specification deferred to v2.2 contract draft.

### 6.5 Worked example: adversarial chain on a migration decision

Input: An engineering team evaluating whether to migrate customer authentication from in-house JWT to a managed provider.

Chain execution:

1. Optimist frame. Migration reduces engineering load (stop maintaining auth code), improves security posture (provider has dedicated security team), enables faster feature shipping (SSO, MFA built-in).
2. Realist frame. Migration cost is real (data migration, integration testing, customer impact during transition). Vendor dependency is introduced. Pricing scales with users and could become expensive at growth.
3. Adversary frame. What if the provider has an outage during peak business hours? What if pricing or terms change unilaterally? What if we need a feature the provider does not support? What if regulatory requirements emerge that the provider does not comply with?
4. Premortem frame. Imagine two years from now this migration is a disaster. Most likely failure modes: (a) migration introduced subtle authentication bugs that hurt user trust; (b) provider outage caused a multi-hour business interruption with material revenue impact; (c) provider pricing increased substantially and switching cost was prohibitive; (d) a needed custom feature the provider would not ship.
5. Mitigation frame. Pre-mitigations addressing (a) through (d): phased rollout (5%, 25%, 100%); abstraction layer making future provider switch easier; SLA negotiation upfront with uptime credits; feature gap audit before commit.
6. Commitment (convergence step with explicit criteria). Proceed if (1) abstraction layer is built first; (2) SLA includes uptime guarantee with credits; (3) feature gap audit shows fewer than three must-have features missing; (4) phased rollout plan agreed by operations team. Otherwise defer.

What this chain produced that single-frame divergence would not:
- Preserved disagreement. Mitigation frame addresses adversary frame's concerns specifically; criteria for commitment are anchored to the failure modes premortem named, not collapsed to a binary verdict.
- Convergence with conditions. Commitment is gated on four specific pre-conditions, not a yes-or-no.
- Audit trail. Each step's output is preserved in the accumulator; a reader can verify the chain visited optimist, realist, adversary, premortem, and mitigation registers in order.
- Falsifiable post-hoc. At a 24-month review, chain validity can be tested by checking whether the named failure modes actually occurred and whether mitigations held.

What this chain did not do:
- Did not assess whether the engineering team's framing about authentication was itself correct. The chain operates within a chosen problem frame; Frame Check at the document or proposal level is a separate operation against the FVS catalog.
- Did not surface frames the team did not consider (regulatory frame; user-experience frame; competitive frame). These would require separate divergence operations.
- Did not enforce that the four commitment conditions are achievable; the team must ground each one separately. The chain's output is the structured commitment with conditions; reality returns confirmation or disconfirmation through grounding (see §9).

## 7. Layer 3: Reality Construct

L3 is the layer where exploration becomes constitutive rather than analytic. Three sub-modes.

### 7.1 L3.1 Ontology shift (Kuhnian paradigm)

The move that shifts the reality categories themselves: from Newtonian mass to relativistic mass-energy; from species-as-fixed to species-as-evolutionary-line. Adjacent territory; v2 acknowledges as part of the wider category but does not claim L3.1 specifically.

### 7.2 L3.2 Constitutive recognition (constructivism)

Seeing what was always already a frame. Money, marriage, property, nation, selfhood as constitutive frames. Once recognized, the frame can in principle be inhabited differently. Adjacent territory; v2 acknowledges, does not claim.

### 7.3 L3.3 Act-instantiation (enactive or pragmatist)

Bringing a divergent frame into reality through act. Acting on a frame changes reality, which changes the receiver (L3.3 -> L0 retroactive loop), which changes which frames are accessible going forward.

L3.3 is claimed in v2 as the strong falsifiable bet on the AGI-era empire anchor. The argument: as AI capabilities scale, L1 simulation and L2 composition commoditize; L3.3 stays irreducibly human (or human-coupled-to-reality) because it requires first-person enactive grounding (acting on frames in acted-upon reality where consequence updates the receiver) that pure cognition without first-person agency cannot replicate. The load-bearing claim is about agency-with-consequence, not biology per se; embodiment is the current human mechanism for it.

Falsification path: if AGI gains equivalent first-person agency in acted-upon reality (robotic embodiment plus consequential acting plus reality-feedback that updates the agent), the L3.3 anchor weakens. Fallback empire moat: catalog with grounded authorship and named lived practice (the curator-authorship anchor; see §11 and §17). The catalog moat survives regardless of trajectory; L3.3 is the additional bet on the irreducibility of first-person enactive grounding for human receivers in the next 5 to 10 year horizon.

Canonical example (operator-grounded): the motorbike. Cognitive verdict was "wasteful." Enactment was required for grounding. The act of riding shifted L0 retroactively (new posture-frame, high-stakes-grounded mode), which made new L1+L2 frames accessible going forward.

## 8. Frame Convergence (first-class lifecycle stage)

Convergence is the operation that takes diverged or chained frame outputs plus criteria and produces a commitment that crosses into action. v1 treated convergence as an implicit chain step; v2 elevates it to a first-class architectural element.

### 8.1 Convergence anatomy

- INPUT: outputs from divergence or chain operations (multiple alternatives, possibly conflicting).
- CRITERIA: themselves frames (typically VALUE-WHY axis activations, sometimes METHOD-axis priorities); criteria are catalog-grounded when possible, surfaced explicitly in all cases.
- DECISION RULE: explicit (multi-attribute weighting, satisficing threshold, lexicographic ordering, max-expected-utility, or other named rule).
- COMMITMENT GRADUATION: full commit, hypothesis-with-revision-points, parallel paths, deferred-commit-with-monitoring.
- DISAGREEMENT HANDLING: when chained frames produce conflicts, the convergence operation explicitly preserves the disagreement and does not collapse it silently; criteria determine whether disagreement resolves to one path or ships as parallel hypotheses.
- OUTPUT: a commitment naming what has been chosen, what has been deprioritized, the criteria applied, and the residual uncertainty.

### 8.2 Convergence mechanics

- Even AGI converges. Capability does not remove the necessity of choosing among alternatives. Convergence is durable across capability scaling.
- Criteria are themselves frames and can be diverged on; meta-convergence is convergence on which criteria-set to apply. v2 does not formally treat meta-convergence; named as future work.
- Convergence is the gateway from cognitive layers (L0 through L2.5) into L3.3 act-instantiation. The commitment exits cognition and enters reality.
- Quality of convergence is measured at GROUNDING (next stage), not at the moment of commitment. A commitment is a hypothesis until reality returns confirmation or disconfirmation.

### 8.3 Decision-theoretic positioning

v2 acknowledges multi-attribute, satisficing, lexicographic, and expected-utility decision rules as legitimate convergence operations. v2 does not commit to a single rule. The chain library can include chain-specific convergence templates (e.g., adversarial-chain default convergence rule: commit if no chain step's adversarial output produces a falsifying counter-case).

### 8.4 Worked example: convergence on alternative product strategies

Input: a product team has surfaced three alternative strategies via multi-stakeholder dialogue (an L2 composite per §5):
- Strategy A: enterprise-first (long sales cycles, high ACV).
- Strategy B: prosumer-first (faster adoption, lower per-user revenue).
- Strategy C: developer-first (slower adoption, defensible if frontier-model commoditization compresses application-layer features).

Criteria as VALUE-WHY frames (per §8.1; criteria are themselves frames):
- C1: revenue per user at 24 months (favors A).
- C2: time-to-market for first paying customers (favors B).
- C3: defensibility against frontier-model commoditization of application-layer features (favors C).

Each criterion is catalog-grounded where possible (citation to relevant literature on enterprise sales cycles; SaaS prosumer adoption rates; AI commoditization patterns).

Decision rule (per §8.3): lexicographic with explicit ordering.
- C3 first because trajectory-contingent and bounds the empire's 5-year horizon.
- C1 second because revenue is a binding constraint on operator-time runway.
- C2 third because time-to-market is recoverable; the other two are not.

Output:
- Commitment: Strategy C (developer-first), with stated criteria ordering.
- Disagreement preserved: A's revenue advantage and B's time-to-market advantage are recorded in the commitment artifact, not collapsed. The team can revisit if C3 falsifies (frontier models do not commoditize application layers as expected).
- Commitment graduation (per §8.1): hypothesis-with-revision-points. The team commits to C with two revision triggers: (1) at 12-month review, if C3's trajectory has not materialized as expected, switch to A or B based on C1/C2; (2) if cash runway shortens to less than 9 months before C delivers revenue, switch to B.

What this convergence produced that unstructured choice would not:
- Criteria-frame relationship is explicit. The team's choice is grounded in three named criteria; future review can audit whether the criteria were the right ones.
- Disagreement is preserved, not collapsed. A and B's advantages are held as live alternatives with revision triggers, not lost.
- Convergence is hypothesis-grade, not certainty-grade. The commitment names what would falsify it.
- Lexicographic ordering forces explicit dominance ranking; multi-attribute averaging would have hidden which criterion does the work.

What this convergence did not do:
- Did not assess whether the three strategies surfaced were the right alternatives to consider. That is upstream divergence work (multi-stakeholder dialogue per §5).
- Did not validate the criteria themselves. Criteria are frames; criteria-on-criteria (meta-convergence) is acknowledged as future work in §8.2.
- Did not enforce that C is achievable. The commitment is a hypothesis; reality returns confirmation or disconfirmation through grounding (see §9).

## 9. Frame Grounding (lifecycle close)

Grounding is the lifecycle stage that follows convergence: the actor commits, acts, and reality returns confirmation or disconfirmation. Grounding is partly out of scope for tooling (it happens in the world, not in MCP calls) but architecturally necessary because:

- Without grounding, convergence is theoretical.
- Grounding outcomes update frame validity (some frames fail in current reality and validate in different ones; the catalog tracks this over time).
- Grounding has timescales (seconds to decades); the catalog's empirical track record is the accumulation of grounding outcomes.
- Grounding closes the loop: confirmed frames retain catalog status; disconfirmed frames are revised or retired (FVS-020 retirement is a precedent per v1 §5.1.4 history).

The MCP tool surface CAN support grounding by accepting feedback on prior chain or convergence outputs, updating frame applicability metadata over time, and surfacing aggregate grounding statistics in the catalog. This is future work; v0 ship does not include grounding-feedback infrastructure.

## 10. Cross-cutting design principles

### 10.1 P1 Divergence serves convergence

Frames are hypotheses until grounded. The lifecycle (detect > diverge > chain > converge > ground) is the unit of value, not any single stage. Quality measured by whether the cycle produces grounded results.

### 10.2 P2 Frames open questions; questions create friction; friction creates novelty

Output of a frame-divergence move is a new question, not a new answer. Question-friction is the primary value measurement. Surface tools producing N comfortable alternatives fail P2. Tools producing fewer perspectives, each generating a question that creates real friction in the user's current understanding, succeed.

### 10.3 P3 Layers have causal loops, not strict hierarchy

L3.3 -> L0 retroactive (acting changes the receiver). L0 -> L1+ gating (state determines accessible frames). Chains can include L3.3 act-and-observe steps interleaved with cognitive frames. The architecture is loops, not stack.

### 10.4 P4 Reflection disrupts practice

High-level frame-divergence is operational, not meta. Forcing reflection imposes cognitive cost that interrupts the live practice. The substrate is rich; the surface must be invisible at the moment of use. Surface tools requiring users to learn the architecture before invoking them fail P4.

### 10.5 P5 Catalog requires grounded authorship, not just citation

Each catalog entry, whether L1 catalog entry (RHETORICAL/CONTENT mode per §4.2 or future axis exemplar), L2 composite, L2.5 chain, or L3.3 grounded act, must carry the public-facing fields: authorship (named, dated), context of testing, failure record, success record, lived-experience anchor, applicability metadata, empirical track record when available. Catalog entries that combine named authorship with grounded operational context provide citation-anchored discipline that scales beyond template-only references; this is the layer of the catalog that supports external citation, peer review, and curator-grade promotion. **This is the primary empire moat regardless of AI capability trajectory.**

Internal operational notes (e.g., friction-cost estimates, per-entry workflow annotations) may accompany catalog entries to support discipline at the curation layer; these are internal artifacts and do not appear in public-facing entry summaries, external citation contexts, or canon-promotion materials.

### 10.6 P6 Construct-honesty extends to construct-safety

See §13 for the forbidden-operations register, divergence-theater detection, convergence-honesty discipline, and L0-state-respecting language.

### 10.7 P7 Three-audience surface design

AI agents (MCP, structured invocations, full architecture exposure, native L0 flex). Flex-humans (rich web tool, architecture visible). Survival humans (radically simplified single-move surfaces; substrate hidden; works AT current L0 without requiring shifts).

### 10.8 P8 Honest scope discipline

Every claim names its evidence basis. Falsifiable predictions are registered. Strong bets are named as strong bets, not facts. The honest-scope statement (§1) is the primary honesty anchor; specific section-level honesty is layered on top.

### 10.9 P9 Structural human-AI asymmetry on identity-detachment

Identity-attachment is the typical baseline tendency in human receivers; identity-detachment is a cultivated state achievable through deliberate practice. AI receivers operate from a different identity-baseline (training-distribution + system frame + RLHF disposition); the architecture-mode register (lower identity defense) is more readily accessible in AI receivers than in human receivers without trained identity-detachment. Architecture work leverages this complementarity: AI sustains the identity-detached register over long sessions; human receivers contribute lived grounding that AI cannot replicate. The principle is operative across multiple spec sections: §3.4 (full statement and L0 context), §12 (three-audience surface design), §17.4 (empire-moat positioning where AI capability scaling is bounded by what AI cannot replicate).

## 11. Catalog discipline

Each catalog entry, regardless of layer, must carry the following public-facing fields:

- **Authorship.** Who authored this entry; named, dated.
- **Context of testing.** In what setting was this exemplar tested.
- **Failure record.** What failed, what surprised, what did not work as expected.
- **Success record.** What worked, with traceable outcome.
- **Lived-experience anchor.** A specific story or case that grounds the entry beyond template.
- **Applicability metadata.** Domain, decision-type, stake-level appropriate.
- **Empirical track record.** Aggregate outcomes when available.

Plus, as internal operational notes (not appearing in public-facing entry summaries, external citation contexts, or canon-promotion materials):

- **Friction-cost estimate.** Cognitive load, time, tokens to invoke. Internal curation-layer signal; supports operator workflow planning and applicability calibration.

The catalog grows by additions that satisfy the public-facing field requirements. Citation-only additions without authored grounding do not enter the catalog at canon-promotion grade.

The lived-experience anchor field carries a separate authorship discipline because of the construct-safety surface area unique to first-person recall. `ANCHOR_AUTHORSHIP_METHODOLOGY_v1.md` specifies the introspective protocol, the corroboration checks, the failure-mode catalog, the construct-honest absence outcome, and the retraction protocol that govern conversion from "Open" to authored anchor. Anchors authored without the methodology's discipline are not at full v2 §11 grade.

### 13.2 Construct-safety (new in v2)

#### 13.2.1 Forbidden-operations register

Operations the contract explicitly does not support, even when technically composable:

- Manipulation chains: predetermined-frame push (chain configured to converge on a chosen output regardless of input).
- Decision-laundering shapes: process applied to legitimize a pre-decided choice.
- Echo-chamber filters: chain configured to reinforce existing frame and exclude challenging frames.
- Exhaustion patterns: long chains designed to produce acquiescence to the most prominent option.
- Frame-planting: chain output that bypasses citation discipline by introducing un-cataloged frames as if cataloged.
- Identity-destabilization: deliberate L0 weakening to bypass defenses (the gaslighting pattern).
- Convergence-forcing: presentation of false consensus or false constraints to force a path.

The contract refuses these operations at the implementation layer; surface tools that compose v2 primitives must implement the same refusals.

Operationalization mapping (each case to detection or refusal mechanism):
- Cases 1 (manipulation chains) and 3 (echo-chamber filters) are detected by §13.2.2 divergence-theater detection. Output diversity per invocation across diverse inputs is the test signal; chains whose outputs do not differ across diverse problems are configured for predetermined-frame push or reinforcement and are refused.
- Cases 2 (decision-laundering) and 7 (convergence-forcing) are refused by §13.2.3 convergence-honesty discipline. Criteria are surfaced explicitly and user-controlled; the operation never claims to have selected for criteria the user did not specify, which prevents both pre-decided choice legitimization and false-constraint forcing.
- Cases 4 (exhaustion patterns) and 6 (identity-destabilization) are refused by §13.2.4 L0-state-respecting language. The tool warns or stops when L0 indicators show defensive activation that would make extended operations exploitative; identity-destabilizing chains are flagged by the same mechanism.
- Case 5 (frame-planting) is refused by §11 catalog discipline plus citation enforcement. Chain output that introduces frames not present in the cited catalog fails the catalog-traceability requirement and is refused at validation.

#### 13.2.2 Divergence-theater detection

Adversarial evaluation: does invoking the operation produce different output than not invoking it? If not, the operation is theater. Built into the test suite for any v2-conformant implementation. Specific tests: output diversity per invocation, novel-question generation rate, friction-detected metric.

#### 13.2.3 Convergence-honesty discipline

Criteria are surfaced explicitly. The user controls them. The convergence operation never claims to have selected for criteria the user did not specify. Disagreement among chained frames is preserved, not silently collapsed.

#### 13.2.4 L0-state-respecting language

Frame-divergence under high stakes can be experienced as attack. Tone and framing differ for users in defensive vs expansive states. The contract honors this; the surface must implement it.

L0 detection methodology is open research per §3.4. v2.1 specifies surface-behavior doctrine where defensive L0 is suspected, with the understanding that detection itself is heuristic until validated:

- Suspected-defensive heuristic signals (text or behavior): short-burst inputs, hurry markers (terse replies, time-stress language), defensive language (challenging the tool, dismissing prior outputs without engaging), repeated requests for the same operation (frustration pattern). For AI receivers (§3.3), suspected-defensive analogs include sycophancy-bias activation, RLHF over-aversion, hedging without commitment, training-distribution caution.
- Suspected-expansive heuristic signals: longer reflective inputs, exploratory questions, willingness to sit with friction, references to multiple frames in a single prompt.
- Surface response under suspected-defensive L0: shorter outputs, lower friction-density per response, single-frame divergence rather than chain or convergence operation, explicit acknowledgment of the user's current frame before introducing alternatives.
- Surface response under suspected-expansive L0: full architecture exposure, multi-axis composites, chains with full convergence, friction-rich question generation.
- Refusal trigger: if defensive L0 is suspected and the user has invoked an exhaustion-pattern operation (very long chain) or identity-destabilization operation (frame-shift that targets the user's self-concept rather than the document), the tool refuses or warns. This is the operationalization referenced from §13.2.1 cases 4 (exhaustion patterns) and 6 (identity-destabilization).
- Honest scope for v2.1: detection signals are heuristic, not validated; refusal triggers err on the side of warning rather than blocking; the user can override in either direction. v2.2 surface contract will specify rendering, tone library, prompt templates, and validated detection methodology when available.
