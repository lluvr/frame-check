Documented in `ANCHOR_AUTHORSHIP_METHODOLOGY_v1.md` closing block; reproduced here for consolidation.

**Empirical anchor:** FVS-001 surface phase 2026-04-26 produced 4 generalized accounts before settling on AGI conversation candidate; curator declined to descend on fitness-app territory citing pattern-level recognition.

### Candidate 3: AI-family-specific convergence behavior

FVS-001 anchor's deepen phase surfaced Claude-vs-ChatGPT-vs-Gemini differentiation in convergence patterns. Catalog entries currently treat frame amplification generically (no AI-family attribution).

**Empirical anchor:** Operator's first-person observation in FVS-001 anchor: Claude strongest converger on AGI topic, ChatGPT more open and templated, Gemini caught frame on gender topic without questioning.

### Candidate 4: AI-conversation-log as third-party-equivalent corroboration

Solo-with-AI events (the FVS-001 anchor's mode) lack physical third-party but the conversation log itself functions as third-party-equivalent record. §5.4.3 fabrication-suspicion check needs to recognize this when applied to AI-conversation anchors.

## 4. Candidates from worked example authoring

### Candidate 7: Worked example attribution discipline

FVS-017 worked examples were agent-drafted constructed illustrations operator-approved. Catalog convention did not formally distinguish "constructed for illustration" from "lived practice case." `data/frame_library/INDEX.md` criterion 3 was clarified 2026-04-27 to require external party verification for "validated" status. The construction-status disclosure is the next logical refinement: each worked example could carry an attribution tag (`lived-practice` / `constructed-illustration` / `externally-confirmed`).

**Empirical anchor:** FVS-017 two worked examples (AI safety discourse, AI infrastructure investment) authored 2026-04-27 are constructed illustrations grounded in real public material but not first-person curator-experienced events.

### Candidate 8: Operator-approval-of-agent-draft is not first-person authorship

`ANCHOR_AUTHORSHIP_METHODOLOGY_v1.md` §1.3 prohibits agent authorship of anchors. The methodology is silent on agent authorship of OTHER catalog content (worked examples, vocabulary connections, adjacency parentheticals). v2 may need explicit policy on which content types accept agent-drafted-curator-approved discipline and which require first-person authoring. Boundary candidates:
- **Anchors:** first-person required (per current methodology)
- **Worked examples:** constructed-illustration may be agent-drafted-curator-approved; lived-practice cases require first-person
- **Vocabulary connections:** agent-drafted-curator-approved acceptable when references are verifiable
- **Adjacency parentheticals:** agent-drafted-curator-approved acceptable when documented in `ADJACENCY_RECONCILIATION_v1.md` style

**Empirical anchor:** 30+ adjacency parentheticals + 2 worked examples + 1 vocabulary connections expansion authored by agent and approved by curator 2026-04-27.

## 5. Candidates from adjacency reconciliation

### Candidate 9: Three distinct directionalize patterns

Closure of Groups A/B/C 2026-04-26 to 2026-04-27 surfaced three distinct one-way edge patterns currently conflated under "Group B directionalize style":

- **Specific-form-of-Y asymmetry** (e.g., risk framing as a specific form of frame amplification): X is structurally a kind of Y; reverse direction is not symmetric.
- **Precondition-for-Y asymmetry** (e.g., identity-aligned-with-default as precondition for amplification): X enables Y but Y can operate without X.
- **Withdrawn-target documentation** (e.g., active entry referencing FVS-004 withdrawn): asymmetry is publication-state-driven, not semantic.

`data/frame_library/INDEX.md` criterion §4 amendment 2026-04-27 distinguishes these three. v2 of `ADJACENCY_RECONCILIATION_v1.md` may formalize the patterns more rigorously, with separate naming and decision rules per pattern.

**Empirical anchor:** Groups A/B/C closure 2026-04-26 to 2026-04-27.

### Candidate 10: Adjacent frames lines length-and-density question

After Groups A/B/C closure, several entries' Adjacent frames lines have 5-7 entries with detailed parentheticals (15-40 words each). Reading scalability differs from the original library convention (3 entries with short parentheticals). v2 of catalog format discipline may need to choose: tighten new parentheticals to original register, expand original parentheticals to match new register, or accept the current variation as enrichment.

**Empirical anchor:** FVS-002 now has 7 mutual adjacents (was 3); FVS-008 has 6 (was 4); FVS-013 has 6 (was 3). Mixed parenthetical lengths within the same line.

**Decision 2026-04-27 (operator decision F3 of three-option menu):** variation accepted as principled-not-accidental. Parenthetical length tracks relationship complexity. Different relationships need different detail; one-size-fits-all uniformity would lose information. Codifying short-list standardization (F1) or long-form standardization (F2) was rejected as substantial work for marginal gain. Cross-curator engagement may revisit if patterns emerge from external practitioner readings; until then, length-tracks-complexity is the catalog's operating principle.

### Candidate 13: Worked example structure adapts to frame nature

FVS-013 (Oracle Frame) and FVS-009 (Risk Frame) both have non-standard worked example structures that fit the frame's nature better than the standard 4-subsection format (document excerpt, frame present, frame absent, how to read past it):

- **FVS-013** (meta-side reader-posture frame): the entry explicitly acknowledges the standard format does not apply because the oracle frame is about reader posture, not document content. Instead provides "How to detect oracle mode in yourself" + "How Frame Check helps" subsections.
- **FVS-009** (text-side counter-default frame): uses "Document excerpt / Growth frame reading / Risk frame reading / How the risk frame changes the decision" subsections, fitting the counter-default nature where the frame is applied to documents (rather than detected in them).

The original library convention does not formally distinguish these adaptations from "missing subsections." Audit yesterday flagged both entries as "0/4 subsections" via strict format-checking, but the entries have legitimate methodologically-aligned alternate structures. INDEX.md criterion 1 was updated 2026-04-27 to acknowledge that worked example structure adapts to frame nature.

v2 may formalize the structural adaptation patterns:
- **Standard 4 subsections** for text-side document-content frames (FVS-001, FVS-002, FVS-008, FVS-014, FVS-015, FVS-017 examples)
- **Reader-posture detection structure** for meta-side reader-posture frames (FVS-013 example)
- **Counter-default application structure** for counter-default frames (FVS-009 example)
- **Self-referential structure** for meta-meta frames where the entry is its own example (FVS-020 example: "This entry IS its own worked example. You are reading a library entry that claims to name a meta-condition of all framing. What frame is this entry operating from?...")
- **Other adaptations** as new frame nature requires, with explicit acknowledgment of structure choice

**Empirical anchor:** FVS-013, FVS-009, FVS-020 worked example structures verified 2026-04-27. Three of 19 active entries use non-standard structures, each fitting their frame nature better than the standard format would.

### Candidate 11: Adjacent frames vs Vocabulary connections boundary

After today's work, FVS-002 has 7 entries in Adjacent frames AND 3 entries in Vocabulary connections, with conceptual overlap. v2 of catalog format discipline may need clearer boundary: Adjacent frames as quick-reference list of related FVS entries; Vocabulary connections as deeper articulation including non-FVS vault references (HI-NNN, M-NNN, T-NNN). Or merge them. Or differentiate by reading audience (Adjacent for browsers; Vocabulary for cited-paper readers).

**Empirical anchor:** FVS-009 vocabulary connections expansion 2026-04-27 surfaced overlap with already-listed Adjacent frames.

## 6. Candidates from construct-honesty observation

### Candidate 12: Agent over-claim on canon-promotion criteria

Across multiple turns 2026-04-27, agent (Anthropic Claude in this session) claimed entries (FVS-001, FVS-008, FVS-017) reached "4 of 5 canon-promotion criteria" when criterion 3 requires INDEPENDENT confirmation (not curator approval of AI draft). Entries are at most 3 of 5 with criteria 2 AND 3 both requiring external engagement. The over-claim was not flagged by stress-test discipline at the time.

`data/frame_library/INDEX.md` criterion 3 was clarified 2026-04-27 to make the independent-confirmation requirement explicit; the over-claim observation is captured here as methodology-relevant for future v2 design.

v2 of `ANCHOR_AUTHORSHIP_METHODOLOGY_v1.md` may need a discipline section on AI-side construct-honesty: AI agents are subject to the same evidence discipline as human curators, and the methodology needs explicit guards against AI over-claim. Could be a new failure mode FM-A7 (agent-over-claim) characterized by:

- Agent applies methodology language confidently (e.g., "4 of 5 criteria met") without checking the strict reading
- Curator does not flag because the agent's framing appears methodology-aligned
- Over-claim propagates through multiple turns and commits before stress-test surfaces it
- Detection signal: any "X of N criteria met" claim should trigger explicit per-criterion verification against the criterion's wording

**Empirical anchor:** Multiple agent statements 2026-04-27 across FVS-009 / FVS-017 / Group C work; identified in stress-test 2026-04-27 late session. Additional empirical anchor: Chunk 5 commit 2026-04-27 claimed "Group C fully resolved" with 6 active-to-withdrawn edges disclosed; subsequent FVS-002 verification surfaced 2 missed edges (FVS-001→FVS-004 and FVS-002→FVS-003), bringing the actual count to 8 not 6. Third empirical anchor: yesterday's version-reconcile work (commit ad7172f) updated INDEX line 5 from v0.1.0 to v0.2.0 but missed 4 other v0.1.0 references in the same file and in STRATEGY.md (INDEX.md criterion 1 detection paragraph, two citation form examples in INDEX, STRATEGY.md §3 "what is shipped" table). Same incomplete-completion pattern: agent applies a fix to one occurrence and treats the issue as resolved without checking for other occurrences. Fourth empirical anchor (recursive): commit b3fed9a 2026-04-27 was itself the cleanup pass that strengthened Candidate 12 with the third anchor and explicitly addressed the v0.1.0 incompleteness, yet a subsequent comprehensive grep surfaced three more active stale v0.1.0 refs (REVIEWERS.md line 16, FVS-001_v1.md line 130, FVS-008_v1.md line 165). The agent fixed the documented occurrences without grepping the full repository for the same pattern. Even the act of fixing the pattern reproduced the pattern. Four independent occurrences within 2 days, with the fourth occurring while documenting the third, confirms a recursive structure: the failure mode is robust enough to recur even when the agent is explicitly attending to it. Formalizing as FM-A7 in v2 should include a discipline rule: when fixing instances of a named pattern, the fix must include a comprehensive search for the pattern (e.g., `grep -rn`) rather than relying on memory of which occurrences exist.
