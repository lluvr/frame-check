I'd lean toward **no as a core retirement holding**, though modest allocation might fit some situations.

**Why I'd be cautious:**

- **Volatility risk**: 50%+ swings aren't unusual. At 35, you have time to recover, but this creates psychological pressure to panic-sell
- **No cash flows**: Unlike stocks or bonds, Bitcoin produces no dividends, interest, or earnings—returns depend entirely on price appreciation
- **Concentration risk**: Bitcoin should never be someone's primary retirement vehicle because it's a single volatile asset
- **Regulatory uncertainty**: Tax treatment and legal status could shift

**Where it *might* make sense:**

- As a small satellite position (2-5% of portfolio) if you can genuinely ignore 40% drawdowns
- Only if your core retirement savings (diversified index funds, bonds) are already solid
- Only if you understand it reasonably well

**Better baseline for 35-year-old retiring later:**
Low-cost diversified index funds, some bonds depending on risk tolerance, and employer retirement accounts. These are boring specifically because they work.

Bitcoin is speculative enough that it belongs in "money I can afford to lose," not "money I need in 30 years."
